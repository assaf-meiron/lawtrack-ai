# LawTrack AI — Customer Discovery Interview Guide

> Ready-to-run guide for validating the [pain points](pain-points.md) with real users. Feeds
> [market research](market-research.md). **Discovery, not a demo** — we're learning, not pitching.

## Goal
Validate (or kill) the reading-tax, renewal-churn, and missed-change pains, and learn how CBA/statute
handling *actually* works today — grounded in a specific recent agreement, not generalities.

## Who to talk to
- **Implementation / onboarding consultant** who reads incoming CBAs and configures policies (primary).
- **HR / compliance admin (customer)** who owns staying compliant through renewals.
- **Sales / pre-sales** on the "do you support our agreements?" moment (short, separate).

## Rules of thumb
- Ask about the **last real agreement** they processed, not "usually."
- **Don't lead** — never pitch LawTrack or name a feature until the wrap-up.
- Chase the expensive step: "walk me through where the *time* actually went."

## Section A — The last agreement (10 min) → P1, P7
- Walk me through the last CBA/CCT you onboarded, from PDF to a configured policy. Step by step.
- How long did it take? Where did the hours actually go — finding the clauses, or deciding what to change?
- How did you know which clauses mattered vs. which to ignore?
- What did you do when a clause didn't map cleanly to a setting?

## Section B — Renewals (8 min) → P2, P3
- When an agreement renews, what do you do? Re-read the whole thing, or diff it somehow?
- Last renewal — how did you find *what changed*? How confident were you that you caught everything?
- Has a change ever slipped through and been caught later? What happened?

## Section C — Missed changes & blast radius (8 min) → P4, P5  ⭐ key wedge
- Tell me about a time a rule was configured wrong because a change was missed. What did it cost?
- When a law or agreement changes, how do you figure out **which customers/policies it affects**?
- How long does producing that "who's affected" list take today?

## Section D — Statutory churn (5 min) → P6
- Which jurisdictions do your customers span? Which reforms are they asking you about right now?
- How do you keep track of statutory changes today? Whose job is it?

## Section E — Trust & the human line (6 min)  ⭐ critical for scope
- If a tool proposed policy changes from a CBA — each with the source clause cited — what would you
  need to see to trust it enough to save time?
- What would make you *distrust* it? (Probe: a confident-but-wrong suggestion.)
- Which decisions would you never let a tool make — even a good one? (effective dates? derogation calls?)

## Section F — Documents (5 min) → feeds document-types
- What formats do agreements actually arrive in? Scanned? Multi-column? Side-letters? Languages?
- Anything about the real PDFs that would trip up an automated reader?

## Section G — Pre-sales (5 min, sales only) → P8
- When a prospect asks "do you support our agreements?", how do you answer today? How long does it take?
- Would a same-day support-matrix change a deal?

## Wrap (5 min)
- If a tool could do **one** thing in this workflow, what would it be?
- Rank the pains we discussed, worst first.
- *(Only now, if useful)* describe the LawTrack concept and watch the reaction.

## After each interview
Log: the last-agreement time breakdown (extract vs map), how renewals are diffed, any missed-change
story + cost, the "who's affected" process + time, the trust bar, document-format surprises, and one
thing that surprised you. Roll into [`market-research.md`](market-research.md).
