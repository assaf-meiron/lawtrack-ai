# Italy — T&A requirements

> **What this is.** The ground-truth reference for Italy's time-&-attendance legal requirements,
> grouped by topic — detailed enough to **build a day.io pay policy from**. It aims to be
> **exhaustive** and **atomic**: **one legal proposition per row**, each row self-contained (no "see
> §X" as the only content), with exact values, a worked example wherever a number is involved,
> variants, and a `Basis` that **links to the primary source** so any row can be checked against the
> law directly.
>
> **Scope: time & day-events only** — money (%, €, tax, gross-to-net) is downstream *context* here
> (kept in `Values` so a policy can be configured; the deliverable is the typed hour/day event).
> **Italian-term convention:** every non-English term is glossed in English in brackets on first use
> in each section.
>
> **The one structural fact that shapes this whole document.** Italy is the archetypal
> **configuration-driven** country: the **statute** (`D.Lgs. 66/2003`) sets only outer guardrails —
> the 40h/48h ceilings, the 11h/24h rest floors, the night definitions, the 250h suppletive OT cap.
> Almost every **operative** number (the actual working week, every *maggiorazione* [premium] band,
> the night window, break length, *banca ore* [hours bank] mechanics, ROL/ex-*festività* accrual)
> comes from the **CCNL** [*Contratto Collettivo Nazionale di Lavoro*, the national sectoral
> collective agreement] — deposited and searchable at the CNEL national archive, not a government
> statute register. "Do we support Italy?" really means "which CCNL applies?" — each CCNL is
> modelled as its own pay policy.
>
> **Legal sources & links:** `D.Lgs. 66/2003` (Testo Unico orario di lavoro) and the Codice Civile
> link to **Normattiva** (normattiva.it), Italy's official legislation database — deep-linked by
> article via its URN scheme, confirmed live. CCNL sector agreements link to the **CNEL national
> CCNL archive** (cnel.it/archivio-contratti), the official deposit register for collective
> agreements (L. 936/1986 art. 17) — confirmed live, but CNEL indexes by sector/code rather than by
> stable per-document URL, so those links point to the archive itself. EU case law links to EUR-Lex.
> 🔎 marks a figure or a deep link not individually re-verified this pass.

## 2. Working-time definition

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **What counts as working time** | Any period during which the worker is **working, at the employer's disposal, and carrying out their activity/duties** (art. 1, c. 2, lett. a). *Reperibilità* [on-call standby away from the workplace] is generally **not** working time except actual call-out hours (see §8). | A worker physically present and directed by the employer counts in full, even during a slow spell. | — | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 1](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art1) |
| **Reference week** | The statutory reference period for the 40h normal week and 48h ceiling is a **fixed 7-day period** — overtime is fundamentally a **weekly** concept, though most CCNL also run a **daily** trigger (see §3a). | — | — | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 3](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art3) |
| **Multiperiodalità [multi-period flexible scheduling]** | A CCNL may authorise **variable weekly schedules** (e.g. 44h peak weeks offset by lighter weeks) provided the average over the reference period stays within limits. Where active, the **planned-hours baseline itself varies by week** — the engine must read planned hours per week from the roster, not a fixed constant. | Peak week rostered at 44h, offset later by a 36h week, keeping the 4-month 48h average intact. | Reference period is 4 months by default, CCNL-extendable to 6/12 months (§4). | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 3](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art3), [art. 4](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art4) |
| **Decimal-hours convention** | Italian payroll expresses part-hours in **decimal**, not sexagesimal: 6h40m is stored/shown as **6.67**. | A 6h40m shift displays as 6.67h on an Italian-facing report/timesheet. | Internal representation may stay in minutes; only Italian-facing output needs the decimal format. | Market convention (payroll software standard) — no statutory basis |

## 3. Overtime — *lavoro straordinario*

*Statute (art. 5, D.Lgs. 66/2003) defines overtime as work beyond normal working time "as defined by law or the CCNL" — in practice, the CCNL sets every operative trigger, band, and cap.*

### 3a. Onset / trigger

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Weekly trigger — the primary statutory notion** | Hours beyond the **contractual normal week** (statute defaults to 40h; most CCNL sit at **38–39h**). This is the primary axis. | 39h-CCNL worker who works 44h in the week has **5h** weekly *straordinario*. | Some CCNL run credit/banking weeks at 37.5h. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 3](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art3), [art. 5](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art5) |
| **Daily trigger — most CCNL also run one** | Hours beyond the normal **daily** schedule (commonly **8h/day**) count as overtime from the first minute over. An hour already counted as **daily** OT must not be double-counted as **weekly** OT. | An 8h-normal-day CCNL worker doing 10h on Tuesday has 2h daily OT; those 2h are excluded from the weekly OT tally at week's end. | Hybrid (day+week) with double-counting protection is the norm; some CCNL run weekly-only. | [CCNL text](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti) (representative) |
| **Part-time — two-tier ladder** | For part-timers: ordinary up to **contract hours** → ***lavoro supplementare*** up to **full-time normal hours** (see §1) → true *straordinario* only **above** full-time normal. | 24h-contract part-timer (full-time = 40h) works 36h: 12h *supplementare*; works 42h: 12h *supplementare* + 2h *straordinario*. | Supplementary volume typically capped ~25% of contract hours by CCNL. | [D.Lgs. 81/2015](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2015-06-15;81) |
| **Overtime requires consent (absent CCNL regulation)** | Where the CCNL doesn't regulate it, OT requires the **worker's consent** and is admissible only for exceptional/*force-majeure* needs; must stay within the 250h suppletive annual cap (§3c). | — | CCNL routinely displaces this with its own authorisation regime. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 5](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art5) |

### 3b. Rate & bands

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ***Maggiorazione* [OT premium] bands — CCNL-tiered** | The CCNL slices OT into **bands** by context (weekday day/subsequent hours, *festivo* [holiday/rest-day], night, night+*festivo*), each its own %. Representative (*Metalmeccanica Industria*): weekday first hours **+25%**, subsequent **+30%**, *festivo* **+55%**, night **+50%**, night+*festivo* **+75%**. | 10h worked on an ordinary Tuesday (8h normal day): 8h ordinary + 2h at **+25%**. Same shift crossing into the night band: those hours are typed at the **combined** night-OT band instead (see §3e). | *Commercio*: diurno +20%/notturno +40%/*festivo* +60%/*festivo*-notturno +70%. *Turismo*: diurno +30%/notturno +60%. *Edilizia*: diurno +35%/notturno +40%/*festivo* +55%. *Credito/ABI*: +25% weekday day/+55% night/+65% *festivo* 🔎. | [CCNL text](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti); representative bands above |
| **Statutory floor — 10%** | No configured OT premium may fall below **+10%**, a minimum still cited from the 1923 decree. | A CCNL band of +8% would breach the floor — validate against it. | — | [R.D.L. 15 marzo 1923, n. 692](https://www.normattiva.it/ricerca/avanzata) 🔎 (URN not individually re-verified this pass) |
| **One band per hour — no double premium** | A given hour takes **exactly one** OT event type, resolved from the (is_overtime, is_night, is_*festivo*) triple against the CCNL band table — never two stacked percentages. | A night hour that's also OT is typed `EXTRA_HOURS_NIGHT_50%`, not `EXTRA_HOURS_25%` + a separate night premium. | See §3e for the general composition rule. | [CCNL](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti) band-table design |

### 3c. Caps & counters

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **250h/year suppletive default annual cap** | Absent a CCNL rule, total overtime per worker is capped at **250 hours/calendar year** — a running YTD counter. | A worker with no CCNL OT clause hits 250h in November → no further OT admissible that year without exception. | CCNL routinely sets its **own** cap, higher or lower: *Metalmeccanica* 200h (>200 employees)/250h (≤200); *Turismo* 260h; *Edilizia* 250h. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 5](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art5) |
| **Approaching/exceeded exceptions** | The engine should raise `OT_ANNUAL_CAP_APPROACHING` / `OT_ANNUAL_CAP_EXCEEDED` against whichever cap (statutory 250h or the CCNL's) applies. | — | — | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 5](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art5); [CCNL](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti) |

### 3d. Averaging & annualisation

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No OT-determining averaging in Italy** | Italy has no annual/period-close netting that *decides* OT pay. The country's one true averaging mechanic — the **48h/4-month** rolling average — validates a **working-time limit** (does the worker's average week stay ≤48h), not OT entitlement; it belongs in §4. | — | *Multiperiodalità* (§2) varies the **weekly baseline** itself, which shifts where the weekly OT trigger (§3a) sits — but this is a per-week baseline change, not a period-close OT netting. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 3](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art3), [art. 4](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art4) (see §4) |

### 3e. Composition & stacking

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Combined bands, not additive stacking** | Night, *festivo*, and OT premiums **do not stack additively** on one hour — the CCNL band table resolves a **single combined** band from the (is_overtime, is_night, is_*festivo*) triple. This is the opposite of Germany's additive *Zuschläge* [surcharges/premiums]. | A night hour that's also an OT hour on a holiday takes the single band `EXTRA_HOURS_FEST_NIGHT_75%` — not three separately-totalled premiums. | Each CCNL defines its own combined-band table; the resolution logic (single band per hour) is structural, not CCNL-optional. | [CCNL band-table design](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti) (representative: *Metalmeccanica*) |
| **Night premium composes independently of OT when the hour isn't OT** | A night hour that is **not** overtime still carries its own night premium (§5) — the "single combined band" rule applies only when the hour is **also** OT. | An ordinary (non-OT) night hour is typed `NIGHT_HOURS` + `NIGHT_PREMIUM_xx%`, separate from the OT band table. | — | [CCNL](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti) (representative) |

## 4. Rest, breaks & working-time limits

*Hard limits → flag a breach, don't silently cap hours.*

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **48h weekly ceiling, averaged over 4 months** | Average working time **including overtime** must not exceed **48h/7 days**, averaged over a rolling **4-month** reference period (CCNL-extendable to **6 or 12 months** for technical/organisational reasons). A health-and-safety cap, not an OT trigger — raise `WEEKLY_48H_AVG_EXCEEDED` rather than a pay event. | Five 50h weeks in a peak quarter are lawful only if offset by lighter weeks so the 4-month average stays ≤48h. | Reference-period extension to 6/12 months is CCNL-set. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 4](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art4) |
| **INL notification duty on a 48h breach** | Where the 48h/4-month average is exceeded, the employer must **notify the labour inspectorate (INL)** — presupposes detecting the breach. | — | — | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 4](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art4) |
| **Effective daily ceiling ≈13h (derived)** | Not a separate statutory number, but the mandatory **11h daily rest** (next row) mechanically caps a working day at **~13h**; some CCNL state this explicitly. | *Edilizia Industria* CCNL states a **≤13h/day** cap directly. | — | Derived from [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 7](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art7); [*Edilizia* CCNL](https://www.cnel.it/archivio-contratti) |
| **Daily rest — 11 consecutive hours** | **≥11h** uninterrupted rest in every 24h period. Reducible only in narrow CCNL-authorised cases, with **equivalent compensatory rest**. | Shift ends 23:00 → next start no earlier than 10:00. | CCNL-authorised derogation cases (e.g. split shifts in hospitality) with comp-rest owed. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 7](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art7) |
| **Weekly rest — 24 consecutive hours, cumulated with daily rest** | **≥24h** rest every 7 days, ordinarily **Sunday**, **cumulated with the 11h daily rest** (**35h total**); averageable over **14 days**. Where Sunday is worked and rest moved elsewhere, that other day becomes the "festive" day for typing purposes — Sunday reverts to an ordinary working day (subject to any CCNL Sunday *indennità* [allowance]). | A worker rostered Sunday with Wednesday as the replacement rest day: Sunday is typed as an ordinary/worked-rest-day hour, Wednesday carries the festive/rest typing. | — | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 9](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art9); [Codice Civile art. 2109](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:regio.decreto:1942-03-16;262) 🔎 |
| **Breaks (*pausa*) — >6h triggers a break** | Daily working period **>6h** → entitlement to a break for recovery/meal, **statutory floor ≥10 minutes**; the **CCNL fixes actual duration and placement**. Breaks are generally **unpaid, not working time**, unless the CCNL/company designates one as paid. | An 8h shift needs ≥10min minimum (CCNL typically sets 30–60min). | `break_is_paid` is a per-break CCNL/company config flag. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 8](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art8) |
| **Minors — 12h daily rest + night ban** | Workers **<18**: **12h** consecutive daily rest (vs. adults' 11h); night ban **22:00–06:00**. | A 17-year-old must clear 12h rest, not 11h, between shifts. | Sector/training exceptions for supervised placements. | [L. 977/1967](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:legge:1967-10-17;977) 🔎 (as amended); [D.Lgs. 345/1999](https://www.normattiva.it/ricerca/avanzata) 🔎 |

## 5. Night & shift work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ***Periodo notturno* [night period] — statutory constraint** | A period of **≥7 consecutive hours that includes 00:00–05:00**; the **CCNL fixes the exact window** inside that constraint. Common windows: **22:00–05:00**, **22:00–06:00**, **24:00–07:00**; hospitality often **24:00–06:00** (some CCNL 23:30–06:30 🔎). | A CCNL setting 22:00–06:00 as the night window: a 21:00–05:00 shift has its 22:00–05:00 portion (7h) flagged as night; the 21:00–22:00 hour is not night. | Window must ⊇00:00–05:00 and span ≥7h — any CCNL window meeting that constraint is valid. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 1](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art1) |
| ***Lavoratore notturno* [night worker] — status test** | A worker is a night worker if they: (1) work **≥3h** of daily working time in the night period **as a normal course**, **or** (2) absent a CCNL rule, work night **≥80 working days/year** (pro-rated for part-time), **or** (3) meet a **CCNL-defined** criterion. | A rotating-shift worker regularly placing ≥3h in the 22:00–06:00 window is a night worker regardless of a day-count. | Both the 3h and 80-day parameters are CCNL-configurable. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 1](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art1) |
| **Night-worker 8h/24h average limit** | Classified night workers: working time must not exceed an **average of 8h per 24h**, over a reference period defaulting to the **working week** (CCNL-extendable). Weekly rest is **excluded** from the average. | A night worker doing a 10h night shift one day must average back down across the week to keep the 8h/24h mean. | Applies **only** to classified night workers — not every worker touching the night window. | [D.Lgs. 66/2003 art. 13](https://www.normattiva.it/uri-res/N2Ls?urn:nir:stato:decreto.legislativo:2003-04-08;66~art13) |
