# Spain — T&A requirements

> **What this is.** The ground-truth reference for Spain's time-&-attendance legal requirements,
> grouped by topic — detailed enough to **build a day.io pay policy from**. It aims to be
> **exhaustive** and **atomic**: **one legal proposition per row**, each row self-contained (no
> "see §X" as the only content), with exact values, a worked example wherever a number is involved,
> variants, and a `Basis` that **links to the primary source** so any row can be checked against the
> law directly.
>
> **Scope: time & day-events only** — money (%, €, gross-to-net) is downstream *context* here (kept
> in `Values` so a policy can be configured; the deliverable is the typed hour/day event).
> **Spanish-term convention:** every non-English term is glossed in English in brackets on first
> use, e.g. *jornada* [working day/hours], *convenio colectivo* [collective bargaining agreement].
>
> **Legal sources & links:** the Estatuto de los Trabajadores [Workers' Statute, "ET"] (Real Decreto
> Legislativo 2/2015) links to its consolidated text on **boe.es** — Spain's official gazette and
> legislation register — per article (`#a34` = art. 34, etc.); RD 1561/1995, RD 2001/1983, RDL
> 8/2019, RDL 5/2023 and RDL 9/2025 likewise link to their boe.es consolidated texts; CJEU cases link
> to eur-lex.europa.eu; Tribunal Supremo (STS) cases link to the CENDOJ case-law search (poderjudicial.es)
> absent a stable direct citation. 🔎 marks a figure or deep link not independently re-confirmed this
> pass (mostly the illustrative convenio figures in the parked appendix, CJEU/STS citations, and the
> two pending-reform items below).
>
> **The one structural fact to hold onto:** the **Estatuto de los Trabajadores** sets only **floors**
> — nearly every operative T&A number (annual *jornada*, overtime route, night *plus* [premium],
> holiday premiums, *bolsa de horas* [hour-bank] size, break rules, tolerance) is actually set by the
> applicable **convenio colectivo** [collective bargaining agreement, sectoral or company-level],
> which may only **improve** on the statute, never worsen it. So many rows below cite the ET as the
> *ceiling-setting* law while the **actual number a policy should type in** comes from the applicable
> convenio (see §1) — "do we support Spain?" really means "which convenio applies?", plus the CCAA
> [autonomous community] / municipal layer for the holiday calendar.

## 2. Working-time definition

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ***Tiempo de trabajo efectivo* [effective work]** | The clock counts only while the worker is **at their post**. Commuting to the workplace and changing into work clothes are **not**, as a rule, effective work; intra-day breaks are **not** effective work unless a convenio/contract says otherwise (§4). | A worker who arrives 10 minutes early to change into a uniform is not automatically owed those 10 minutes as work, absent a convenio rule. | Long-standing employer *tolerance* of counting a break does not by itself create a vested right (STS case law). | [ET art. 34.5](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |
| **Cross-midnight shifts split at 00:00 for classification** | A shift spanning midnight (e.g. 21:00→05:00) is **split at 00:00** for night/Sunday/holiday classification, while kept as **one continuous stretch** for the 12h between-shift rest test. | An 18:00→02:00 shift yields 4 night hours (22:00–02:00) split across two calendar days for holiday/Sunday purposes, but the shift itself counts as one block for inter-shift rest. | No statute forces the split; it follows from the clock-time night window and calendar-day holiday/Sunday tests. | [ET arts. 34.1, 36.1, 37.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430) (derived) |
| ***Calendario laboral* [annual labour calendar]** | Employer publishes annually, per worker/group: scheduled workdays, annual *jornada*, weekly-rest pattern, and which of the ≤14 holidays are paid. This is the **planned-day source of truth** the engine measures excess/shortfall against. | — | — | [ET art. 34.6](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |
| **Travel time — no fixed workplace** | For workers with **no fixed or habitual workplace**, travel time between home and the **first/last client** counts as working time; computation starts at the first centre served for those with a fixed base. | A field-service technician's drive from home to the first customer visit is compensable working time. | Contrast workers with a fixed workplace, whose ordinary commute does not count. | [CJEU C-266/14 (*Tyco*)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:62014CJ0266) 🔎; [STS 27-May-2013 (CENDOJ)](https://www.poderjudicial.es/search/indexAN.jsp) 🔎 |

## 3. Overtime

*Spain's overtime is **annualised**: a long day/week is only ever "candidate" overtime — it first flows into the annual balance / *bolsa de horas* [hour-bank] (§7), and true `horas extraordinarias` [overtime] crystallise only at year-end reconciliation, or when a convenio sets its own earlier daily/weekly trigger.*

### 3a. Onset / trigger

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No clean daily/weekly OT trigger — annual reconciliation** | Overtime = effective hours beyond the **annual** ordinary *jornada* set by convenio/contract. A long day is a **candidate** excess posted to the *bolsa* (§7); it converts to confirmed overtime only if it survives to the annual close, or a convenio defines its own earlier trigger. | A 10h Tuesday (2h over an 8h plan) posts +2h to the *bolsa*; it becomes `HORAS_EXTRAORDINARIAS` only if the annual balance is still positive at year-end (or the convenio triggers OT weekly/daily). | Convenios frequently **do** add an explicit daily/weekly OT trigger on top of the annual reconciliation — a profile switch. | [ET art. 35.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |
| **Overtime must be defined against the *convenio*/contract ceiling, not statute** | The "ordinary maximum" that overtime sits above is whatever the applicable convenio/contract fixes (commonly 1,700–1,800h/yr), not the 1,826h statutory reference. | A worker under a convenio with a 1,750h/yr ceiling starts accruing OT-candidate hours past 1,750h, not past the 1,826h statutory figure. | — | [ET art. 35.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |
| **Part-timers barred from overtime** | Part-time workers cannot legally accrue overtime (force-majeure excepted); excess hours are *horas complementarias* instead (§9), never `HORAS_EXTRAORDINARIAS`. | A 25h/week contract worker doing 30h in a week: the 5h route through the complementary-hours mechanism, not OT. | — | [ET art. 12.5](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a12) |
| **Night workers barred from overtime** | A *trabajador nocturno* [night-worker status, §5] cannot legally do overtime at all (force-majeure aside). | — | — | [ET arts. 36.1, 35](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |
| **Minors barred from overtime** | Workers <18 may never be assigned or perform overtime. | — | — | [ET art. 6.3](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a6) (via youth-protection regime) |
| **Force-majeure overtime is mandatory** | *Fuerza mayor* [force-majeure] overtime — to prevent/repair accidents and urgent extraordinary damage — is compulsory (unlike voluntary "ordinary" OT) and still compensated, but is excluded from the 80h annual cap (§3c). | A flood-response overtime shift is compulsory and doesn't count toward the annual OT ceiling. | — | [ET art. 35.3](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |

### 3b. Rate & bands

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No statutory OT %; pay ≥ ordinary-hour value** | The law requires only that each overtime hour be paid **no less than** the value of an ordinary hour (or compensated with equivalent rest, §3c) — no fixed statutory uplift %. The exact % is convenio-set. | — | Common convenio uplifts run well above the floor (illustrative only; money is out of this reference's scope). | [ET art. 35.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |

### 3c. Caps & counters

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **80 overtime hours/year — hard annual cap** | A per-worker running **YTD counter capped at 80h/year**, reduced proportionally for part-year/part-time. | A worker approaching 75h of confirmed OT in October should flag before the 80h ceiling. | Pro-rated for shorter-than-standard annual *jornadas*. | [ET art. 35.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |
| **Rest-compensated OT (within 4 months) drops off the 80h cap** | Overtime routed to compensatory rest, **taken within 4 months**, does **not** count toward the 80h annual cap; force-majeure OT is likewise excluded. | 10h of OT taken as time off within the 4-month window never touches the 80h counter. | Absent a pact, the **default route is rest**, not pay (§7). | [ET art. 35.1, 35.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |

### 3d. Averaging & annualisation

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Annual jornada reference decides OT, not a weekly/daily window** | The whole OT model hinges on netting the year's daily/weekly peaks and troughs against the convenio's annual *jornada* figure; a heavy week is not itself an OT event. | A worker running 5 extra hours in week 12 may see it fully offset by a light week 30, netting to zero at year-close. | A convenio may add its own **daily/weekly OT trigger** running in parallel with the annual reconciliation. | [ET art. 34.1, 35.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430) |
| **Irregular-distribution excess feeds the same annual test** | Excess/defect posted to the *bolsa de horas* (§7) under the irregular-distribution mechanism is netted against the annual *jornada*; uncompensated excess (beyond the permitted % or past the compensation window, through employer fault) becomes **disguised overtime** [*hora extraordinaria encubierta*]. | A *bolsa* balance of +12h still open at the 12-month window's close, through employer fault, converts to confirmed `HORAS_EXTRAORDINARIAS`. | Window and % are convenio-adjustable (statutory floor 10% / 12 months, §7). | [ET art. 34.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) (derived doctrine) |

### 3e. Composition & stacking

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Base-vs-overlay: one base bucket, parallel premium overlays** | Every effective hour gets exactly **one** base classification (ordinary / overtime / *complementaria* / *festiva* / *presencia*) but can **simultaneously** carry up to three parallel overlays — night (*nocturna*), Sunday (*domingo*), holiday (*festivo*) — each counted separately, never re-bucketing the base hour. | An hour worked 23:00–00:00 on a Sunday that is also a public holiday, beyond the annual *jornada*: base = overtime **+** overlays night, Sunday, holiday — four separate quantities on one hour. | — | Doctrine derived from [ET arts. 34–37](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430) (no single codifying article) |
| **The *bolsa de horas* stores raw hours, no multiplier** | Unlike a phase-multiplier bank, Spain's hour-bank posts **plain +/− hours**; any rate uplift is money handled entirely downstream. | +3h posted for a heavy Saturday carries no built-in premium — it's a flat hour credit. | — | [ET art. 34.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |

## 4. Rest, breaks & working-time limits

*Hard limits → flag a breach, don't silently cap hours.*

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Daily ordinary ceiling — 9h** | No more than **9 effective ordinary hours/day**, unless a convenio/works-rep agreement redistributes daily hours differently (always subject to the 12h inter-shift rest, below). This is a **safety limit, not an OT trigger** — crossing it flags a breach; it doesn't by itself create overtime. | A 10h day breaches the 9h ceiling unless the convenio permits redistribution. | Minors: firm 8h/day, no redistribution. | [ET art. 34.3](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |
| **Between-shift daily rest — 12h** | **≥12h** between the end of one *jornada* and the start of the next — holds **even under irregular distribution**. | Shift ends 22:00 → next start no earlier than 10:00. | Special sectors (RD 1561/1995): transport/shift-changers may drop to a floor of **7h**, with the shortfall made up in following days. | [ET art. 34.3](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34); [RD 1561/1995](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-1995-21346) |
| **Weekly rest — 1.5 days, accumulable over 14 days** | **≥1.5 uninterrupted days/week**, accumulable across periods of up to **14 days**. Default pattern: Saturday afternoon (or Monday morning) + all Sunday. | A worker owed 1.5 days weekly can bank a lighter week 1 and take 3 days off in week 2, as long as the 14-day window nets ≥3 days total. | Many convenios improve to **2 full consecutive days** (common in hospitality). Minors: **2 full uninterrupted days**, no averaging. | [ET art. 37.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a37) |
| **Cumulative daily + weekly rest — ~48h consecutive** 🔎 | Recent doctrine holds the 12h daily rest and the weekly rest **cumulate rather than overlap**, implying **≥48 consecutive hours** (12h + 36h) of combined rest at the week boundary. | A shift ending Friday 22:00 followed by weekly rest starting Saturday: the worker is owed the 12h inter-shift rest (until 10:00 Saturday) **plus** the 36h weekly-rest block, i.e. ≥48h before the next shift can start. | Applies CJEU C-477/21 (*MÁV-START*) reasoning; exact Spanish ruling citation unconfirmed — flagged for verification. | 🔎 [STS (2026)](https://www.poderjudicial.es/search/indexAN.jsp) applying [CJEU C-477/21 (*MÁV-START*)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:62021CJ0477) |
| **Intra-day break — ≥15min when continuous work > 6h** | Trigger: continuous *jornada* **exceeding 6 hours** → **≥15-minute** break. Exactly 6.0h does **not** trigger it absent a more generous convenio. Minors (16–17): **≥30 minutes** once continuous work exceeds **4.5h**. | An 8h continuous shift owes ≥15min; a 5h shift owes none. | Whether the break counts as **effective work** is a per-convenio `break_counts_as_effective` flag — statutory default is **not effective** (unpaid, subtracted). | [ET art. 34.4](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |
| **Split shift (*jornada partida*)** | A split shift always produces **two punch blocks**; the mid-day gap is **unpaid** and non-effective unless pacted otherwise. | 12:00–16:00 + 20:00–00:30 with a 16:00–20:00 unpaid gap (common hospitality pattern). | Convenio may pact the gap as paid/compensated. | [ET art. 34.4](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) (derived); convenio |

## 5. Night & shift work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Night window — 22:00–06:00** | Every hour inside the window is a night hour, regardless of whether the whole shift is nocturnal. A convenio may **widen** the window (worker-favourable), **never narrow** it. | An 18:00→02:00 shift yields **4 night hours** (22:00–02:00). | Some provincial texts define a narrower *plus* [premium] band than the statutory protection band — the statutory 22:00–06:00 governs the 8h-cap/OT-ban rules regardless; the convenio band governs only the premium overlay. | [ET art. 36.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |
| ***Trabajador nocturno* [night-worker] status** | A worker who **normally works ≥3h of the daily jornada** in the night window, **or** is expected to work **≥1/3 of the annual jornada** at night, gains night-worker status. | A rotating-shift worker regularly on 20:00–04:00 (6h nightly, well above the 3h test) is a night worker. | — | [ET art. 36.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |
| **Night-worker rolling 8h/day average — 15-day window** | Night workers are capped at an **average ≤8h/day over a rolling 15-day reference period** (not a hard daily cap). | A night worker doing 10h one night must offset with shorter nights across the 15-day window. | — | [ET art. 36.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |
| **Night workers barred from overtime** | Absolute (force-majeure aside) — see §3a. | — | — | [ET art. 36.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |
| **Max 2 consecutive weeks on night shift, absent volunteering** | In 24/7 shift operations, a worker may not be rostered to nights for **more than 2 consecutive weeks** unless they volunteer. | A worker rostered to nights for weeks 1–2 must rotate to a day/evening shift in week 3, unless they've expressly volunteered to stay on nights. | — | [ET art. 36.3](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) (derived) |
| ***Plus de nocturnidad* [night premium]** | **No statutory %** — set by convenio, unless the salary was already fixed on the basis the job is inherently night work, or compensated with rest instead of pay. | Night hours in a hospitality convenio commonly carry a convenio-set uplift (money — out of this reference's scope). | Compensation route (pay vs rest) is convenio-set, same as overtime (§7). | [ET art. 36.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |
| **Minors — absolute night-work ban** | Workers <18 may **never** be assigned night work (22:00–06:00). | A 17-year-old cannot be rostered for any part of a 20:00–04:00 shift that falls inside the 22:00–06:00 window. | — | [ET art. 36.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a36) |

## 6. Weekend & public-holiday work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **≤14 paid, non-recoverable public holidays/year** | National holidays + CCAA holidays + **≤2 local** holidays, jurisdiction-keyed (CCAA + municipality), **changing yearly**. Sourced from the *calendario laboral*. | *Assumption Day* observed only where the CCAA chooses it as a substitute for a national date; a local *fiesta* [town holiday] varies by municipality. | If a CCAA can't fit its traditional holiday within 14, it may add **1 extra recuperable** [make-up] holiday. | [ET art. 37.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a37) |
| **Holiday cannot be absorbed by weekly rest — 2024/2025 doctrine** | When a rotating **weekly-rest day coincides with a public holiday**, the worker cannot simply lose the holiday — compensation is owed (substitute rest day, per convenio, or economic). | A worker whose rotating rest day lands on a national holiday is owed a substitute day (or pay) rather than the holiday quietly disappearing. | Compensation **form** (substitute day vs economic) is convenio-dependent. | 🔎 [STS 997/2024 (*Espasa Calpe*); STS 372/2025 (*Zara*)](https://www.poderjudicial.es/search/indexAN.jsp) |
| **Working a holiday / weekly-rest day → compensatory rest or ≥75% uplift** | Absent a more favourable convenio rule: when a holiday or weekly rest **must exceptionally be worked**, the employer grants **compensatory rest** or pays the hours worked **increased by ≥75%** (money — out of scope; the engine records only the hour quantity + route). | A worker called in on a public holiday: route = REST → compensatory-rest liability opens; route = PREMIUM → downstream applies the ≥75% uplift. | Many hospitality convenios instead let holiday hours **accumulate and be enjoyed later** as rest at a time the worker chooses. | [RD 2001/1983 art. 47](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-1983-20906#a47) |
| **Sunday work — premium only where a convenio sets one** | The statute does **not** by itself make Sunday a premium day; a consequence attaches only where the applicable convenio provides one (very common in retail, where commercial Sunday opening is regulated). | A retail convenio attaches a Sunday premium; an office-sector convenio may attach none. | — | Convenio-dependent — no ET default |

## 7. Time banking & time-off-in-lieu

*Two distinct banks coexist and must not be merged.*

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Irregular distribution — statutory 10%/annual jornada, no pact** | Absent convenio/works-rep agreement, the employer may unilaterally distribute up to **10% of the annual jornada** irregularly, with **≥5 days'** notice, respecting the 12h daily / 1.5-day weekly rest floors. | An employer schedules a peak week beyond the plan, staying within the 10% annual allowance. | Convenios routinely raise the %: e.g. Grandes Almacenes allows **up to 15%**. | [ET art. 34.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |
| ***Bolsa de horas* [irregular-distribution bank] — the running ledger** | Each day posts `+(worked−planned)` or `−(planned−worked)`; the running balance compensates within **12 months** absent a convenio-set window. Measured in **plain hours, no multiplier**. | A retail worker banks +3h on a sales-period Saturday, to be offset by a lighter day within the 12-month (or convenio) window. | Convenio may lengthen/shorten the compensation window and raise the %. | [ET art. 34.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) |
| **Disguised overtime — excess uncompensated in time / beyond the %** | Excess beyond the permitted % **or** left uncompensated through employer fault becomes *hora extraordinaria encubierta* [disguised overtime] → converts to confirmed `HORAS_EXTRAORDINARIAS`, feeding the 80h cap (§3c). | A +12h *bolsa* balance still open past the 12-month close, through employer fault, converts to overtime. | — | [ET art. 34.2](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a34) (derived) |
| **OT-to-rest bank — 4-month clock, default route absent a pact** | Absent an agreement to pay overtime, the **default compensation route is equivalent paid rest, taken within 4 months** of the overtime being worked. Rest taken in time **drops the hours off** the 80h annual cap (§3c). | 10 confirmed OT hours converted to 10h of rest, taken within 4 months — never touches the 80h counter. | Convenio/contract may instead pact **pay** as the route. | [ET art. 35.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a35) |
| **The two banks are distinct and cannot be merged** | *Bolsa de horas* (irregular distribution, 12-month window, plain hours, netted against the annual jornada) and the **OT-to-rest bank** (confirmed overtime only, 4-month window, feeds the 80h cap) are separate ledgers with different triggers, windows, and consequences. | A worker can simultaneously carry a +5h *bolsa* balance **and** a 6h OT-to-rest liability — they don't net against each other. | — | [ET arts. 34.2, 35.1](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430) |
| **Holiday/night-work compensatory-rest accrual** | Where the convenio routes a holiday-worked or night-work premium to **rest instead of pay**, it opens the same kind of compensatory-rest liability as OT-to-rest (§6, §5), commonly with the convenio's own accrual/expiry rule rather than the 4-month OT clock. | A hospitality worker's holiday hours accumulate and are enjoyed later at a time the worker chooses. | Convenio-set window and form (day off vs money). | Convenio-dependent — no single ET article |

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |

| **Complementary hours reported to worker representatives** | *Horas complementarias* worked are reported to worker representatives **annually** (convenio-dependent cadence, e.g. Grandes Almacenes). | — | Cadence set by convenio. | [ET art. 12.5](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2015-11430#a12) (derived); convenio |

# Spain — T&A compliance support

**Verdict: 🟠 Partial → the weakest fit of the three.** Spain's *entire* OT architecture is **annualised**

## Governing sources — who actually sets the rules

The compliance answer is conditional — the operative T&A numbers usually live **below** the national
statute, in the **convenio colectivo** (the statute sets only floors); "do we support Spain?" really
means "**which convenio applies?**" (plus the CCAA/local resolution for the holiday calendar).

| Layer | Instrument | Sets the operative T&A numbers? |
|---|---|---|
| EU | WTD 2003/88/EC | No — outer envelope only |
| Statute (floor) | Estatuto de los Trabajadores (RDL 2/2015) arts. 12/34–38 | **Floor only** — national minima (40h/wk annual-avg, 9h daily ceiling, 80h/yr OT cap, night 22:00–06:00, 12h inter-shift, 1.5-day weekly rest, ≤14 holidays, complementarias caps) |
| Statute (special) | RD 1561/1995 (jornadas especiales) | Floor for special sectors — tiempo de presencia, reduced rest |
| Statute (record) | RDL 8/2019 (art. 34.9) | Record obligation — registro de jornada, 4-yr retention (+ pending digital decree, not in force) |

| Sector convenio | State / provincial, REGCON-registered | **Yes — THE operative layer** (jornada anual, nocturnidad %, festivo/Sunday premiums, complementarias %, OT-pay-vs-rest, irregular-distribution %) |
| Company convenio | Company-level agreement | Yes — improves further on the sector convenio |
| Case law | STS / CJEU | Interpretive — reshapes doctrine (e.g. rest, holiday-on-rest) |
