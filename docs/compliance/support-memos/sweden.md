# Sweden — T&A requirements

> **What this is.** The ground-truth reference for Sweden's time-&-attendance legal requirements,
> detailed enough to **build a day.io pay policy from**. It aims to be **exhaustive** and **atomic**:
> **one legal proposition per row**, each row self-contained (no "see §X" as the only content), with
> exact values, a worked example wherever a number is involved, variants, and a `Basis` that **links
> to the primary source** so any row can be checked against the law directly.
>
> **Scope: time & day-events only** — money (%, SEK, tax, gross-to-net) is downstream *context*
> here (kept in `Values` so a policy can be configured; the deliverable is the typed hour/day
> event). **Term convention:** every non-English term is glossed in English in brackets on first
> use in each section, e.g. *övertid* [overtime].
>
> **The one structural fact that shapes this whole document.** Sweden's **Arbetstidslagen** [Working
> Hours Act, ATL, 1982:673] sets working-time *limits* (daily/weekly hours, rest, breaks, on-call
> ceilings) but — like Germany's ArbZG — puts **no statutory premium or onset rule on overtime**:
> those come almost entirely from **kollektivavtal** [collective agreements], which cover ~90% of the
> Swedish workforce even without a legal extension mechanism. The statute is also **near-fully
> derogable by central kollektivavtal** (ATL §3) down to an EU-Working-Time-Directive floor — so
> **the operative numbers for OT, night/weekend premiums, and time banking live below the statute**,
> per bargaining unit (see §1). Read every OT/premium/banking row here as "statute sets the outer
> limit; the CBA sets the number."
>
> **Legal sources & links:** primary statute text via [lagen.nu](https://lagen.nu/) for
> **Arbetstidslagen** (1982:673), **Semesterlagen** [Annual Leave Act, 1977:480], **Lag om sjuklön**
> [Sick Pay Act, 1991:1047], **Föräldraledighetslagen** [Parental Leave Act, 1995:584],
> **Socialförsäkringsbalken** [Social Insurance Code, 2010:110] ch. 12–13, **Lag om ledighet för
> närståendevård** [1988:1465], **Studieledighetslagen** [1974:981], and **Lag om stöd vid
> korttidsarbete** [2013:948]; plus Arbetsmiljöverket [Swedish Work Environment Authority] guidance
> (AFS 2023:2/2023:3 ch. 8, minors), union guidance (Unionen, Kommunal, Vision, Ledarna) for typical
> kollektivavtal premium structures, and government/press sources for 2026 changes. Full list at the
> foot of the requirements section. lagen.nu section anchors confirmed live (2026-07-21) at pattern
> `https://lagen.nu/<SFS-nr>#P<§>S1`; 🔎 marks a link or figure not independently re-verified in this
> pass.

## 1. Scope, classification & governing sources

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Statute sets limits, not pay** | Arbetstidslagen [Working Hours Act, ATL] governs max hours, rest, breaks, on-call ceilings — **no statutory OT premium, no statutory OT onset**. Both are 100% kollektivavtal [collective agreement] / individual contract. | A non-unionized employer with a silent contract owes only the **ordinary** hourly rate for extra hours worked. | ~90% of Swedish employees are covered by a kollektivavtal (no legal extension mechanism — coverage is via employer association membership or company-level accession). | [ATL (1982:673)](https://lagen.nu/1982:673); Medlingsinstitutet coverage stats 🔎 |
| **Kollektivavtal = one pay policy per agreement** | Each central agreement (e.g. Kommunal's municipal agreement, Unionen/Almega white-collar agreements, IF Metall's industrial agreements) sets its own OT premium formula, OB-tillägg [inconvenient-hours premium] bands, working-time-account rules, and leave top-ups. Model each as a **separate** policy. | Kommunal (municipal/regional employers): OT ≈ 180% of hourly wage for the first 2h, 240% after. Almega healthcare (white-collar, e.g. E&F agreement): simple OT = monthly salary ÷ 94/hour; qualified OT = monthly salary ÷ 72/hour. | Riksavtal [industry-wide central agreement] vs lokalt kollektivavtal [local/company agreement]; agreements can also be *hängavtal* [single-employer accession to an existing sector agreement]. | Kollektivavtal per union/employer-association pairing — [Kommunal](https://www.kommunal.se/ob-overtid-forskjuten-arbetstid) 🔎 |
| **§3 ATL — central kollektivavtal may derogate the statute** | A central agreement concluded/approved by a national employee organisation may deviate from **§§5–6, 7(2), 8–10b, 12–14, 15(2)–(3)** — i.e. ordinary hours, on-call, overtime/extra-time caps, averaging, rest, and part of breaks — down to (never below) the EU Working Time Directive (2003/88/EC) floor. Local agreements may deviate from a narrower set (§§8–8a, 9(2), 10–10a, 13(2)) for **≤1 month** from signing. | A sector CBA extends the reference period for the 48h weekly-average cap, or reduces daily rest to the EU floor of 11h with different scheduling. | Absent a CBA, Arbetsmiljöverket [Work Environment Authority] may itself grant a dispensation under §19 "if there are special reasons," subject to the same EU floor. | [ATL §3](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P3S1), [§19](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P19S1) |
| **§2 ATL — statutory scope exclusions** | The Act does **not** apply to: (a) work where the employer cannot supervise hours worked; (b) employees in a **företagsledande eller därmed jämförlig ställning** [managerial/executive position] or who otherwise control their own working hours; (c) work in the employer's **household** [domestic work]; (d) **seafaring** work; (e) road-transport work covered by the separate Working Time (Road Transport) Act (2005:395). | A managing director who freely sets their own hours is outside ATL entirely — assign no ATL-based limit policy. | Domestic workers and seafarers have their own separate statutory regimes. | [ATL §2](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P2S1) |
| **Ordinary full-time hours — 40h/week** | Default **statutory ceiling 40h/week**; may be averaged over **up to 4 weeks** if the nature of the work requires it. Actual full-time norm is very often lower via CBA (e.g. 37.5–38.75h/week common in white-collar/public-sector agreements). | A CBA setting 38.75h/week as "full-time" makes hours 39–40 already extra time under that agreement, even though ATL's own ceiling is 40h. | CBA-set full-time hours are the practical baseline most policies should use, not the statutory 40h. | [ATL §5](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P5S1) |
| **Deltid [part-time] — onset baseline = contractual hours** | For a part-time worker, extra hours are counted from their **contracted** hours, not the 40h/statutory full-time line (this feeds *mertid*, §3b). | A 25h/week contract worker doing 30h has 5h of mertid, not overtime. | — | [ATL §10](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P10S1); contract |
| **Timanställning [hourly/casual employment]** | No separate statutory regime — hours are simply counted per engagement; CBA typically sets a minimum call-out/shift length (see §9 non-adjacent-overtime minimum). | — | Common in retail/hospitality CBAs. | Kollektivavtal |
| **Minors — three protected age bands** | **Under 13**: work is **in principle prohibited** (narrow exceptions: light/safe family-business tasks, or special Arbetsmiljöverket permit). **13 to <16** ("barn" [child]): restricted light work only. **16–17** (completed compulsory school, "ungdom" [youth]): broader work allowed but still capped (hours in §4, night ban in §5). | A 14-year-old may do light seasonal work with parental consent and Arbetsmiljöverket rules; a 12-year-old generally may not be employed at all. | Family-business light-task exception; special permits. | [AFS 2023:2/2023:3 ch. 8 §§8–23](https://www.av.se/en/work-environment/publications/rules-and-regulations/) 🔎 (regulation numbering under revision) |
| **⚠ Pending — shorter working week reform** | A political proposal (Socialdemokraterna) for a 6-hour day / 4-day week / extra leave, with a **state-funded 5,000-employee pilot** floated for roll-out from **2030**, contingent on the **September 2026 election**. **Not yet law — do not model as settled.** | — | — | Political proposal only (2026) 🔎 |

## 2. Working-time definition

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Jourtid [on-site on-call] — counts in full** | Time an employee **must be present at the workplace** ("stå till arbetsgivarens förfogande på arbetsstället") to perform work if needed. Counts in full as working time / against the jourtid cap (§8). | A hospital doctor required to remain on-site overnight has that whole period as jourtid. | Distinguished from *beredskap* below by statute text itself (§14). | [ATL §6](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P6S1) |
| **Beredskap(stid) [off-site standby/reachability]** | Time the employee may spend **away from the workplace** but must be reachable/available to work if called. **Not** counted toward jourtid/OT caps or toward weekly rest — only actual worked call-outs count as working time. | An engineer on a beredskap rota can be anywhere, but if called at 2am to fix a system, only the call-out minutes count as worked time. | CBA sets any standby allowance for the "waiting" hours (money, downstream). | [ATL §14](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P14S1) (by contrast with jourtid, §6) |
| **Restid [travel time]** | No general statutory rule; CBA/contract typically defines whether business travel outside ordinary hours is compensated (often at a reduced rate or as separate restidsersättning [travel-time compensation]) rather than folded into working time. | — | Highly CBA-dependent (e.g. construction/consulting agreements have explicit restidsersättning clauses). | Kollektivavtal 🔎 (no statutory basis) |
| **Missing/undocumented time** | No statutory default; employer records actual hours (§11 ATL) — an unrecorded gap is a data/process issue, not a legal presumption. | — | — | [ATL §11](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P11S1) |

## 3. Overtime

*Sweden, like Germany, has no statutory overtime premium — onset premium and rate are entirely CBA/contract-defined. The statute only caps the total volume of "extra time" categories.*

### 3a. Onset / trigger

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Övertid [overtime] — statutory definition** | Hours worked **beyond ordinary working hours** (§5, ordinarie arbetstid) or **beyond scheduled hours**, when ordered/necessary and not already offset by jourtid. No statutory premium attaches — only the volume caps in §3c apply. | A 40h-week (ATL ordinary) employee working 45h has 5h övertid; whether it carries a premium is 100% CBA/contract. | CBA typically defines its **own** "ordinary hours" baseline (e.g. 38.75h), shifting the onset point earlier than the statutory 40h. | [ATL §7](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P7S1)–[§8](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P8S1) |
| **Mertid [part-time extra time]** | For part-time (deltid) workers: hours **beyond the individual's contracted hours** but still within the CBA/statutory full-time ceiling. Distinguished from övertid, which starts only once full-time ordinary hours are exceeded. | 25h-contract worker doing 32h in a 38.75h-full-time CBA: 7h is mertid, not övertid (no övertid until >38.75h). | — | [ATL §10](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P10S1) |
| **OT must be ordered / necessary** | Extra time must be **ordered by the employer or be necessitated by unforeseen circumstances** — not simply self-initiated by the worker. | An employee choosing to stay 2h without instruction generally has no claim to övertidsersättning. | CBA may require prior authorisation/logging. | [ATL §8](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P8S1); kollektivavtal |

### 3b. Rate & bands

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No statutory OT premium** | ATL sets **no** pay uplift. Typical CBA structure (**Kommunal**, municipal/regional blue-collar, illustrative): ≈**180%** of hourly wage for the first 2h of a day, **≈240%** thereafter. | Monthly salary irrelevant here — Kommunal's OT is an hourly-wage multiplier: 2h at 180% + 1h at 240% on a 150 SEK/h base = 540 + 360 = **900 SEK**. | Rates vary materially by sector CBA; some agreements express OT purely as a % of hourly wage on ordinary weekday/other-time bands. | Kollektivavtal ([Kommunal](https://www.kommunal.se/ob-overtid-forskjuten-arbetstid)) 🔎 exact % vary by agreement year |
| **Almega white-collar OT — divisor model** | **Almega/Vårdföretagarna (e.g. E&F agreement)**: "enkel övertid" [simple overtime, weekdays 06:00–20:00 adjoining ordinary hours] = monthly salary ÷ **94** per hour; "kvalificerad övertid" [qualified overtime, weekdays 20:00–06:00 or any weekend/holiday hour] = monthly salary ÷ **72** per hour — a distinct rate-construction rule from Kommunal's %-of-hourly-wage model above, not a variant of it. | Monthly salary 32,000 SEK: 2h enkel övertid = 32,000÷94×2 ≈ **681 SEK**; 4h kvalificerad övertid = 32,000÷72×4 ≈ **1,778 SEK**. | The enkel/kvalificerad split and divisors vary by sector CBA and wage-round year. | Kollektivavtal (Almega/Vårdföretagarna) 🔎 |
| **Non-adjacent overtime — minimum-hours floor** | Where OT is not directly adjoining the ordinary shift (a gap larger than a meal break), CBA commonly pays a **minimum of 3 hours** even if less was worked. | Called in for 1h of unplanned OT with no adjoining shift → paid as if 3h worked. | CBA-specific minimum-call floor. | Kollektivavtal ([Unionen guidance](https://www.unionen.se/rad-och-stod/regler-overtidsersattning-och-kompensationsledighet)) 🔎 |

### 3c. Caps & counters

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **General övertid cap** | **≤48h per rolling 4 weeks**, or **≤50h per calendar month**, and **≤200h per calendar year** (allmän övertid [general overtime]). | An employee at 45h in one 4-week window is within the general cap; a second month pushing the running annual total past 200h breaches it — a running YTD counter. | — | [ATL §8](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P8S1) |
| **Extra övertid [additional overtime]** | A further **≤150h/year** ("särskild övertid") permitted only where special circumstances exist and no other solution is reasonably available; **combined** general+extra volume in any 4-week/month window still can't exceed the 48h/50h ceiling. | A seasonal spike justifies dipping into the 150h extra pool, but the same 48h/4-week ceiling still binds week-to-week. | Requires either a central-CBA basis or, absent one, individual employer justification (subject to Arbetsmiljöverket oversight). | [ATL §8a](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P8aS1) |
| **Nödfallsövertid [emergency overtime]** | **Uncapped** — permitted for unforeseen events (natural disaster, accident, safety-critical failure) with no standard hour ceiling, but must be reported to Arbetsmiljöverket after the fact. | A flood damaging critical infrastructure justifies open-ended emergency work. | — | [ATL §9](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P9S1) |
| **Mertid cap (part-time)** | **≤150h/year** ("extra mertid") under special circumstances, subject to the same combined 48h/4-week or 50h/month ceiling as övertid. | A 25h/week contract worker with a 100h mertid total so far this year can still be assigned mertid up to the 150h annual cap, provided the 4-week/month combined ceiling isn't breached in any given window. | — | [ATL §10a](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P10aS1) |
| **Jourtid cap** | **≤48h per employee per rolling 4 weeks**, or **≤50h per calendar month** — a separate running counter from övertid. | A worker with 40h jourtid in a 4-week window has 8h of remaining jourtid headroom that period. | — | [ATL §6](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P6S1) |

### 3d. Averaging & annualisation

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Total-working-time averaging (§10b) — validates the limit, not OT pay** | **Total working time** (ordinary + övertid combined) must average **≤48h/week** over a reference period of **up to 4 months**. This is a working-time-*limit* check (also placed at §4 per the template's convention that limit-validating averaging goes there) — it is listed here too because it directly bounds allowable OT volume within the period. No OT premium is netted at period close — it is a hard ceiling on total hours, not a pay-determining average. | A worker at 55h one week must be offset by lighter weeks so the 4-month rolling average stays ≤48h. | CBA (central) may extend the reference period, subject to the EU Directive floor. | [ATL §10b](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P10bS1) |

### 3e. Composition & stacking

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **OB-tillägg [inconvenient-hours premium] and OT stack additively** | Where a CBA defines both an OB-tillägg (evening/night/weekend/holiday premium, §6) and an OT premium, they are typically tracked as **separate typed buckets** and paid **additively** on the base rate — an OT hour worked at night on a weekend can carry base + OB + OT premium, each totalled separately. | A Kommunal employee's Saturday-night overtime hour: base wage + storhelg [major-holiday]/weekend OB + the 180%/240% OT multiplier, computed as distinct components. | Some CBAs instead apply a single combined multiplier (no separate OB) for OT hours — de-pyramiding rules are agreement-specific. | Kollektivavtal (varies by agreement) 🔎 |

## 4. Rest, breaks & working-time limits

*Hard limits → flag a breach, don't silently cap hours.*

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ordinary weekly hours — 40h, averageable over 4 weeks** | **≤40h/week**, averageable over **up to 4 weeks** where work nature requires it. | A 45h week is lawful only if offset within the 4-week window to ≤40h average. | CBA commonly sets a lower "ordinary hours" full-time baseline (37.5–38.75h). | [ATL §5](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P5S1) |
| **Total working time — 48h/week average over ≤4 months** | Ordinary hours + övertid combined must average **≤48h/week** over a reference period of **up to 4 months** (see §3d). | A worker doing 55h one week and 41h the rest of a 4-month period must land at or below a 48h/week average across that whole window, even though no single week is capped at 48h. | Central CBA may extend the period (EU floor applies). | [ATL §10b](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P10bS1) |
| **Dygnsvila [daily rest] — 11h per 24h period** | **≥11 consecutive hours** of rest in every 24-hour period, and this period must **include midnight–05:00** ["nattperioden," the night period] except where operational needs require otherwise. | A shift ending 22:00 → next start no earlier than 09:00. | Central/local CBA may shorten toward the EU Directive floor (11h) with compensating rest; certain services (e.g. named essential sectors) get statutory exceptions to the midnight–05:00 inclusion. | [ATL §13](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P13S1) |
| **Nattarbetare [night worker] — 8h/24h average cap** | A night worker (regularly working ≥3h of their daily hours in the night period, or a substantial part of their hours at night) is capped at an **average of 8h per 24h over 4 months**; workers in **hazardous/high-strain roles** may not exceed **8h in any single night shift** (no averaging). | A rotating night-shift worker with heavy physical/mental strain duties is limited to a firm 8h that specific night, not just on average. | Employer must also arrange health assessments for regular night workers (general Arbetsmiljölagen [Work Environment Act] duty). | [ATL §13a](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P13aS1) |
| **Veckovila [weekly rest] — 36h consecutive** | **≥36 consecutive hours** of rest **each week**, as far as possible over the weekend (Sat/Sun). **Beredskapstid [off-site standby]** does **not** count against/interrupt this rest period. | A worker resting Sat 18:00–Mon 06:00 clears the 36h; an off-site standby rota over that window doesn't break the rest. | Central/local CBA may adjust scheduling of the 36h within the EU floor. | [ATL §14](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P14S1) |
| **Raster [breaks] — after no more than 5h continuous work** | Employees may not work **more than 5 consecutive hours** without a rast [break] — an unpaid stoppage during which the employee is free to leave the workplace. Rasters may, where necessary, be replaced by a **måltidsuppehåll** [meal pause] taken at the workplace, which — unlike a rast — **counts as working time**. | An 8h shift must include at least one break before the 5h mark; if operational needs prevent a true rast, a paid on-site meal pause substitutes. | Number/length of breaks per day is otherwise CBA-set (no fixed statutory minutes beyond the 5h trigger). | [ATL §15](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P15S1)–[§16](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P16S1) |
| **Pauser [short pauses]** | Short work pauses (distinct from rasters) may be required as needed for health/safety (e.g. screen-work, physically strenuous tasks); these **count as working time**. | A data-entry role builds in short paid pauses through the shift. | Set by risk assessment / CBA, no fixed statutory minutes. | [ATL §17](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P17S1); general Arbetsmiljölagen duty |
| **Minors — night ban & extra rest (school-age, 13–<16)** | **13–<16 / school-age minors**: night ban **20:00–06:00**; **≥14h** daily rest; max **7h/day** during school breaks, **2h/day** on school days (max **12h/school week**); **≥2 days/week** (36h continuous) weekly leave; **4 weeks** continuous annual leave. | A 15-year-old can't be rostered past 20:00 on a school-week day even where an adult CBA allows evening shifts. | Conditional exceptions for supervised/educational tasks under mentorship; combined study-work programmes (PRAO, apprenticeships) count school + work hours together toward the weekly cap. | [AFS 2023:2/2023:3 ch. 8 §§16, 18–23](https://www.av.se/en/work-environment/publications/rules-and-regulations/) 🔎 |
| **Minors — hours & rest (16–17, "ungdom")** | **16–17 (completed compulsory school)**: max **8h/day, 40h/week**; **≥12h** daily rest (reducible to **11h** with compensation); free from work **22:00–06:00 or 23:00–07:00** (with sector exceptions); **≥2 days/week** (36h) weekly leave. **All minors** (both bands): no work **00:00–05:00**; **≥30min** continuous rest after **4.5h** worked. | A 17-year-old apprentice caps at 8h/day even on an adult CBA rostering 10h shifts. | Conditional exceptions for hazardous-task exposure (height >3.5m, machinery) under supervised mentorship. | [AFS 2023:2/2023:3 ch. 8 §§16, 18–23](https://www.av.se/en/work-environment/publications/rules-and-regulations/) 🔎 |

## 5. Night & shift work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Night period (statutory)** | **00:00–05:00** must fall within the 11h dygnsvila [daily rest] window (§13); the broader **nattperiod** used for night-worker status assessment is not itself pinned to a single clock window in the statute — CBA/context determines what counts as "night" hours for premium purposes (commonly 22:00–06:00 or 20:00/21:00–06:00 in practice). | A shift running 20:00–06:00 satisfies the §13 test (it fully spans 00:00–05:00) even though a CBA might separately define "night" as starting at 22:00 for premium purposes. | 🔎 confirm the practical night-premium window per CBA — it is not the same as the §13 rest-inclusion window. | [ATL §13](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P13S1) |
| **Nattarbetare [night worker] status & cap** | Regular/substantial night-period work → capped at **avg 8h/24h over 4 months**; hazardous-role night workers capped at a firm **8h/shift**, no averaging (see §4). | A rotating night-shift worker in a hazardous role is limited to a firm 8h that specific night, not just on average. | — | [ATL §13a](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P13aS1) |
| **OB-tillägg natt [night premium]** | **No statutory %/SEK.** CBA-typical (Kommunal-style blue-collar): roughly **90–140 SEK/hour** for major-holiday ("storhelg") night/weekend work, **≈50–70 SEK/hour** for ordinary weekend work, **≈25–40 SEK/hour** for evening work — figures vary by agreement and are revised in annual wage rounds. | A Kommunal care worker's Friday-night shift carries the weekend-band OB rate on top of ordinary pay. | Rates differ materially between Kommunal (hourly SEK add-on) and salaried white-collar agreements (which may instead reduce the divisor for OT, see §3b) — always confirm the specific CBA's current table. | Kollektivavtal ([Kommunal](https://www.kommunal.se/ob-overtid-forskjuten-arbetstid)) 🔎 exact SEK bands vary by year/agreement |
| **Schema/skiftform [shift-pattern allowance]** | Continuous 3-shift/rotating-roster premiums are CBA-set (no statutory rate); often layered with OB-tillägg rather than replacing it. | — | Common in manufacturing/24-7 healthcare operations. | Kollektivavtal 🔎 |

## 6. Weekend & public-holiday work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Helgdagar [public holidays] — national calendar** | **11 fixed-date + moveable holidays**: Nyårsdagen [New Year's Day, 1 Jan], Trettondedag jul [Epiphany, 6 Jan], Långfredagen [Good Friday], Påskdagen [Easter Sunday], Annandag påsk [Easter Monday], Första maj [1 May], Kristi himmelsfärdsdag [Ascension, 39 days after Easter], Pingstdagen [Whit Sunday], Sveriges nationaldag [National Day, 6 Jun], Midsommardagen [Midsummer Day, Sat between 20–26 Jun], Alla helgons dag [All Saints, Sat between 31 Oct–6 Nov], Juldagen [Christmas Day, 25 Dec], Annandag jul [Boxing Day, 26 Dec]. All **Sundays** are also statutory "röda dagar" [red days] but of lesser weight than the named holidays. | Midsummer Day always falls on a Saturday — no substitute weekday granted. | *Julafton* [Christmas Eve], *Nyårsafton* [New Year's Eve] and *Midsommarafton* [Midsummer Eve] are **not** official holidays but are near-universally treated as non-working "klämdagar" [bridge days between a holiday and a weekend]-adjacent days by custom/CBA. | [Public holidays in Sweden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_holidays_in_Sweden) 🔎 (no single consolidating statute; observed nationally) |
| **No statutory holiday-work prohibition** | Unlike Germany, Sweden's ATL does **not** generally ban Sunday/holiday work outright — CBA and sector practice govern whether/when staff are rostered on red days. | Retail, healthcare, and hospitality routinely roster on Sundays/holidays under sector CBA terms. | — | [ATL](https://lagen.nu/1982:673) (silent); kollektivavtal |
| **OB-tillägg helg/storhelg [weekend/major-holiday premium]** | **No statutory %/SEK** — see §5 figures; "storhelg" [major holiday, e.g. Christmas/New Year/Midsummer/Easter] commonly carries the **highest** OB band (illustrative ≈90–140 SEK/hour under Kommunal-style agreements), above the ordinary weekend band. | A Christmas Day shift pays the storhelg OB rate, higher than an ordinary Sunday shift. | CBA-specific storhelg date lists and rates. | Kollektivavtal ([Kommunal](https://www.kommunal.se/ob-overtid-forskjuten-arbetstid)) 🔎 |
| **Monthly-salaried holiday pay (no deduction)** | A holiday falling on what would have been a working day is paid as normal (built into the monthly salary; no separate holiday-worked doctrine needed for non-worked holidays). | — | Hourly/timanställda workers' holiday pay depends on the CBA (may be built into the hourly rate or paid as a supplement). | Kollektivavtal/employment contract 🔎 |

## 7. Time banking & time-off-in-lieu

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Kompensationsledighet [compensatory time off for OT]** | Many CBAs let övertid be converted to time off instead of cash, at a set ratio. Illustrative (Unionen-guidance figures): **enkel övertid** [simple OT, weekday daytime] → **1.5h off per 1h worked**; **kvalificerad övertid** [qualified OT, evenings/weekends] → **2h off per 1h worked**. | 4h of kvalificerad övertid converts to **8h** of comp leave. | Ratio and eligible OT categories are entirely CBA-defined. | Kollektivavtal ([Unionen guidance](https://www.unionen.se/rad-och-stod/regler-overtidsersattning-och-kompensationsledighet)) 🔎 |
| **Arbetstidskonto [working-time account]** | A CBA-established running per-worker account banking worked hours (credit) against time off, extra pension contribution, or cash payout (debit) — the specific cap and menu of uses is agreement-specific. Illustrative: one municipal-sector agreement grants **63h** of banked leave/year distributable at the employee's choice, plus up to **9h** employer-allocable, **separate** from the 25-day statutory annual leave. | An employee banks hours through the year, then chooses to take extra paid leave, add to pension, or cash some out. | Structure, caps, and payout rules vary widely by CBA — some sectors have no arbetstidskonto at all. | Kollektivavtal ([Vision, Sobona municipal-company agreement](https://www.vision.se/rad-stod/arbetstid/arbetstidskonto)) 🔎 |
| **Arbetstidsförkortning [working-time reduction, ATF]** | Some CBAs convert part of an annual wage increase into **banked reduced-hours time** rather than pay, functioning like a time-bank feeding extra leave days. | — | Sector-specific (notably some industrial-sector agreements). | Kollektivavtal 🔎 |
| **Korttidsarbete [state-supported short-time work]** | A temporary, state-co-financed **reduction of working hours** during economic crisis, at three tiers: **20%** hours cut (12% pay cut), **40%** cut (16% pay cut), **60%** cut (20% pay cut). State reimburses **43%** of the calculated support cost (skills-development time reimbursed at **60%**, capped). General support runs up to **12 months**, extendable once for another 12; hardship support up to 6 months (extendable +3); aggregate cap **24 months per 36-month period**. | A plant cuts hours 40% for 6 months: staff work 60% of normal hours for 84% of normal pay, state co-funding part of the gap. | Requires local/central agreement basis or individual-employee consent. | [Lag (2013:948) om stöd vid korttidsarbete](https://lagen.nu/2013:948) |
| **Korttidsarbete reshapes the expected-hours baseline** | Because a korttidsarbete reduction changes the *planned* schedule itself (not an absence from it), the engine must treat the reduced hours as the new expected shift for that period — similar to Germany's Kurzarbeit — rather than logging the unworked portion as a leave/absence event. | A worker on a 40%-cut korttidsarbete plan has their expected shift recalculated to 60% of normal hours; no absence event is generated for the missing 40%. | — | [Lag (2013:948)](https://lagen.nu/2013:948) |

## 8. On-call & standby

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Jourtid [on-site on-call]** | Employee **must remain at the workplace**, available to work if needed — counts **in full** toward working time and the jourtid cap (**≤48h/4 weeks or ≤50h/month**, §3c). | A hospital's on-call physician staying on the ward overnight accrues full jourtid hours. | Pay for jourtid hours is CBA-set (often a reduced rate vs. active work). | [ATL §6](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P6S1) |
| **Beredskap(stid) [off-site standby]** | Employee may be **away from the workplace** but must be reachable/available; counts as **rest time** for ATL purposes (does **not** interrupt veckovila, §14) — only actual **worked call-out minutes** count as working time. | An on-call IT engineer sleeping at home, woken for a 45-minute remote fix, has only those 45 minutes as working time. | CBA sets a standby allowance (a fixed SEK/hour "waiting" rate) for the beredskap period itself — money, downstream. | [ATL §14](https://lagen.nu/1982:673#P14S1) |

## 9. Scheduling & premium pay

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No statutory reporting/predictability pay** | Sweden has **no** statutory show-up / reporting-time / predictability-pay regime (comparable to Germany). Roster-change compensation and notice periods are entirely CBA-defined. | — | — | (none statutory); kollektivavtal |
| **Förskjuten arbetstid [shifted working time]** | Where an employer reschedules a worker's shift **with short notice** (illustrative CBA threshold: **within ~10 days** of the shift), a förskjuten arbetstid supplement is commonly owed instead of/alongside OT rules; a **same-day** change instead typically triggers ordinary OT rules for any hours beyond the original schedule. | A shift moved from days to evenings 5 days ahead triggers the förskjuten arbetstid premium, not an OT rate. | Notice thresholds and the premium itself are CBA-specific. | Kollektivavtal ([Kommunal](https://www.kommunal.se/ob-overtid-forskjuten-arbetstid)) 🔎 |
| **Non-adjacent OT minimum-hours floor (scheduling view)** | Where a call-in is not directly adjoining the ordinary shift (a gap larger than a meal break), CBA commonly pays a **minimum of 3 hours** even if less was worked (same rule as §3b, restated here as the scheduling event it triggers). | Called in for 1h of unplanned work with no adjoining shift → paid as if 3h worked. | CBA-specific minimum-call floor. | Kollektivavtal ([Unionen guidance](https://www.unionen.se/rad-och-stod/regler-overtidsersattning-och-kompensationsledighet)) 🔎 |

## 10. Leave & absence

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Semester [statutory annual leave] — accrual & entitlement** | **25 semesterdagar** [vacation days] per **semesterår** [vacation year, **1 Apr–31 Mar**], accrued during the preceding **intjänandeår** [qualifying year, same 12-month window by default]. Paid days = (employment days in the qualifying year − unpaid-absence days) ÷ total days in the qualifying year × 25, rounded **up**. Employees starting **after 31 August** get only **5 days** for that first (short) year. | Joined 1 Nov → only 5 statutory days until the next full semesterår. Worked the full qualifying year with 30 unpaid-leave days → paid days = (365−30)/365×25 ≈ 22.9 → rounds up to **23**. | CBA typically grants **25–30+ days** (many white-collar agreements: 25 up to a certain age, rising to 30–32 for older employees); "sammanläggning" [concurrent qualifying/vacation year] can be agreed instead of the staggered default. | [Semesterlagen §3](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P3S1), [§4](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P4S1), [§7](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P7S1) |
| **Huvudsemester [main summer leave] — 4 consecutive weeks Jun–Aug** | Unless otherwise agreed, leave must be scheduled to give the employee **≥4 consecutive weeks during June–August**. | An employer must offer a 4-week block within the summer window absent a differing agreement or special circumstances. | Waivable by agreement; "särskilda skäl" [special circumstances] can justify deviation. | [Semesterlagen §12](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P12S1) |
| **Leave-scheduling notice** | Employer must notify the leave decision **≥2 months** before it starts (or **≥1 month** if special circumstances shorten the runway). | A leave block starting 1 September must normally be communicated to the employee by 1 July; a late-arising staffing gap can compress that to a 1 August notice under the 1-month exception. | CBA may set different notice windows. | [Semesterlagen §11](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P11S1) |
| **Semesterlön [holiday pay] — sammalöneregeln (monthly-paid)** | **Sammalöneregeln** [same-salary rule, for monthly-paid staff]: current salary + fixed supplements, plus a **semestertillägg** [holiday supplement] of **0.43% of monthly salary** per day (weekly-paid: **1.82% of weekly salary** per day) + **12%** of variable pay accrued in the vacation year. | A monthly-paid employee on 32,000 SEK taking 5 days gets 5× (32,000/21.75-ish ordinary-day value) + 5×0.43%×32,000 as the semestertillägg component, on top of ordinary salary continuation. | — | [Semesterlagen §16a](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P16aS1) |
| **Semesterlön [holiday pay] — procentregeln (no fixed wage)** | **Procentregeln** [percentage rule, for those without a fixed monthly/weekly wage, e.g. hourly staff]: **12%** of total qualifying-year pay — a distinct calculation method from sammalöneregeln above, applied to a different worker population. | An hourly worker earning 180,000 SEK across the qualifying year accrues 12% × 180,000 = **21,600 SEK** of semesterlön. | — | [Semesterlagen §16b](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P16bS1) |
| **Sparad semester [saved/carried-over leave]** | Days **above 20 paid days/year** may be saved and used within **5 years**. | An employee with 30 CBA days takes only 20 one year; the extra 10 can roll for up to 5 years. | — | [Semesterlagen §18](https://lagen.nu/1977:480#P18S1) |
| **Sjuklön [statutory sick pay] — employer-paid period** | Employer pays sick pay for the **first 14 calendar days** of each sjukperiod [sick-pay period] (day 1 = onset of reduced work capacity + next 13 days), at **80%** of pay/benefits. | An employee sick 8 days is paid sjuklön for all 8 days by the employer, at 80% of pay (subject to the karensavdrag deduction below). | — | [Lag om sjuklön §6](https://lagen.nu/1991:1047#P6S1), [§7](https://lagen.nu/1991:1047#P7S1) |
| **Karensavdrag [qualifying deduction] — proportional, once per sick period** | A **karensavdrag** equal to **20%** of an average week's sjuklön is deducted **once per sick-pay period** (not a fixed "first day unpaid" — a proportional deduction since the 2019 reform). After **10 karensavdrag within a rolling 12 months**, no further deduction applies for that employee. A new illness beginning **within 5 days** of a prior period's end is treated as a **continuation** (combined ≤14-day employer period). | An employee sick 3 separate days across a week has a single ~20%-of-weekly-sjuklön deduction taken proportionally, not three separate "day 1 unpaid" hits. | **Högriskskydd** [high-risk protection] — chronically ill employees can get the karensavdrag waived (employer reimbursed by Försäkringskassan). | [Lag om sjuklön §6](https://lagen.nu/1991:1047#P6S1) |
| **Sjukanmälan & läkarintyg [reporting & medical certificate]** | Days **1–6**: no documentation required (self-certification, "skriftlig försäkran" [written assurance]). **Day 7+**: a doctor's/dentist's certificate is required. Pay runs only from the date of notification (or the illness date if the worker was prevented from reporting). | An employee off sick 8 days must produce a certificate covering days 7–8 onward. | CBA/employer policy may require a certificate earlier ("first-day certificate," a common employer-level rule, not statutory). | [Lag om sjuklön §8](https://lagen.nu/1991:1047#P8S1), [§9](https://lagen.nu/1991:1047#P9S1) |
| **Sjukpenning [state sick benefit] beyond day 14** | Försäkringskassan [Social Insurance Agency] takes over from day 15 — money/eligibility mechanics downstream. | An employee still sick on day 15 moves off the employer's sjuklön and onto Försäkringskassan-paid sjukpenning for the remainder of the sjukperiod. | — | [Socialförsäkringsbalken](https://lagen.nu/2010:110) (sjukpenning chapters) 🔎 |
| **Graviditetspenning [pregnancy benefit]** | Paid leave before mammaledighet where a physically demanding/risky job cannot be adjusted or reassigned — available from **as early as 60 days before** the due date, at **≈80%** of SGI [sjukpenninggrundande inkomst, benefit-qualifying income]. | A warehouse worker unable to be reassigned from heavy lifting starts graviditetspenning ~2 months before the due date. | — | [Socialförsäkringsbalken ch. 10](https://lagen.nu/2010:110) 🔎 |
| **Mammaledighet [maternity leave, birth parent]** | **Mandatory**: at least **7 weeks before** and **7 weeks after** birth (combined **≥2 weeks** is compulsory rest immediately around birth). | Due 1 June → protected leave window opens ~mid-April. | — | [Föräldraledighetslagen §4](https://lagen.nu/1995:584#P4S1) |
| **Hel ledighet [full parental leave]** | Right to full leave until the child is **18 months old**, extendable while the parent draws **full föräldrapenning** [parental benefit] beyond that. Adoptive parents: **18 months from placement**. | A parent takes continuous leave from birth to 18 months, then extends via benefit days. | — | [Föräldraledighetslagen §5](https://lagen.nu/1995:584#P5S1) |
| **Föräldrapenning [parental benefit] — total days & levels** | **480 days total per child** (more for multiples): **390 days** at **sjukpenningnivå** [sickness-benefit level, ≈**77.6%** of SGI, capped at an income ceiling of **10 prisbasbelopp** [price base amounts]] + **90 days** at a flat **lägstanivå** [minimum level, fixed SEK/day 🔎 current rate]. **90 days per parent are non-transferable** ("reserved"/quota days) — the remainder can be transferred between parents. Usable until the child turns **12** (full-day use generally until age **4**, reduced-scope thereafter). | Two-parent family: each parent has a non-transferable floor of 90 days; the remaining 300 days can be allocated between them as they choose. | Single parent gets the full 480 days themselves. | [Socialförsäkringsbalken ch. 12](https://lagen.nu/2010:110) 🔎 |
| **Delledighet [part-time parental leave] — with benefit** | With föräldrapenning: reduce to **3/4, 1/2, 1/4, or 1/8** of normal hours while drawing the matching partial benefit. | A parent works 75% hours while drawing 1/4-level föräldrapenning. | — | [Föräldraledighetslagen §6](https://lagen.nu/1995:584#P6S1) |
| **Delledighet [part-time parental leave] — without benefit** | **Without** benefit: reduce by up to **1/4** of normal hours until the child turns **8** or completes 1st grade — a separate, unconditional right from the benefit-linked reduction above. | A parent unilaterally drops to 75% hours (no benefit drawn) until the child finishes 1st grade. | — | [Föräldraledighetslagen §7](https://lagen.nu/1995:584#P7S1) |
| **10-dagar [10 partner/co-parent days at birth]** | **10 days per child** (20 for twins, 30 for triplets, scaling per additional child) for the parent **not giving birth** (or another eligible relative in specified circumstances) — usable from birth through **60 days after the child comes home**; paid at **≈80%** of SGI (max 7.5 prisbasbelopp), applied for within 30 days of first day home. | A father takes his 10 days in the first two months post-birth, largely overlapping the mother's mammaledighet. | Twins/triplets scale the day count. | [Socialförsäkringsbalken ch. 13](https://lagen.nu/2010:110) (tillfällig föräldrapenning vid barns födelse) 🔎 |
| **Tillfällig föräldrapenning / VAB [temporary parental benefit — sick-child care]** | **120 days per child per year** (general cap), paid at a rate similar to sjukpenningnivå, to care for a sick child (typically ages 0–12, extended for children with ongoing extra needs). **2026 update**: three new qualifying situations added — instructing/training school or preschool staff on a child's self-care needs; attending meetings on the child's condition/disability; participating in social-services assessments of the child's support needs. | A parent takes a VAB day when a school-age child has the flu. | Extended-use rules for children with disabilities/chronic illness (higher effective caps). | [Socialförsäkringsbalken ch. 13](https://lagen.nu/2010:110); 2026 amendment (VAB expansion) 🔎 |
| **Omvårdnadsbidrag-linked reduced hours** | Up to **1/4** hours reduction while the child receives **omvårdnadsbidrag** [care allowance for a child with a disability/chronic illness]. | A parent of a child receiving omvårdnadsbidrag drops from 40h to 30h/week (a 1/4 reduction) without losing employment rights. | — | [Föräldraledighetslagen §9](https://lagen.nu/1995:584#P9S1) |
| **Närståendevård [leave to care for a seriously ill close relative]** | Full or partial (**1/4, 1/2, 3/4**) leave corresponding to the **närståendepenning** [relative-care benefit] level drawn; benefit payable for up to **100 days total per relative** (raised from 60 in the 2010 reform). Applies to a child, parent, spouse/registered partner, or household member; requesting flexible arrangements requires **≥6 months'** employment. | An employee takes reduced hours to care for a dying parent, drawing partial närståendepenning. | — | [Lag (1988:1465) om ledighet för närståendevård §3](https://lagen.nu/1988:1465#P3S1), [§6](https://lagen.nu/1988:1465#P6S1), [§7](https://lagen.nu/1988:1465#P7S1) |
| **Studieledighet [study leave]** | Right to leave for education after **6 months' current employment** or **≥12 months total in the past 2 years** (waived for union-related training). Employer may **postpone** (not deny) the leave, with immediate written notice of reasons; longer postponements need local union consent where a CBA applies. No statutory pay continuation — reinstatement to an equivalent position is guaranteed. | An employee hired 4 months ago does not yet qualify (needs 6 months' current tenure) unless they can show ≥12 months total employment across the past 2 years, e.g. via a prior stint with the same employer. | — | [Studieledighetslagen (1974:981) §3](https://lagen.nu/1974:981#P3S1)–[§5](https://lagen.nu/1974:981#P5S1), [§8](https://lagen.nu/1974:981#P8S1)–[§9](https://lagen.nu/1974:981#P9S1) |
| **Holiday-pay indemnity on termination** | Accrued but untaken semesterdagar (and their semesterlön) are paid out in full on termination of employment. | — | — | [Semesterlagen](https://lagen.nu/1977:480) (general principle) |
