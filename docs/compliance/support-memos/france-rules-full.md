## ➕ Candidate in-scope rules (#36–#52) — detailed

All rows here are **➕ candidates** (not ground truth). Each carries a plain-language **What it means**
explanation researched this session; the **capability** and **verdict** cells are ⚠ **provisional

| # | Rule | What it means — plain-language explanation | Our current capability (⚠ prov.) | Verdict (⚠ prov.) | Basis | Scope |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 36 | **RTT / JRTT** (jours de réduction du temps de travail) | The company keeps a working week above 35h (e.g. 39h) but, instead of paying the extra hours as overtime every week, it "repays" them as whole days off spread across the year — enough days that the *average* week lands back at 35h. The day-count is set either "réaliste" (counted from the weeks actually worked over 35h) or "forfaitaire" (a fixed number/yr in the accord). So RTT is a device to *avoid* overtime, which is what distinguishes it from RCR (#9) — RCR converts overtime that has *already* triggered into rest. | Banked-hours / rest-in-lieu can approximate; no automatic JRTT accrual counter | 🟠 | F34151; loi 2008-789; company accord | in-scope (A) · M |
| 37 | **Aménagement pluri-hebdomadaire / cycles / modulation** | The employer may measure the week not week-by-week but *averaged* over a longer "reference period" — 2 to 52 weeks (up to 3 years if a branch agreement allows). Busy weeks over 35h and slack weeks under it cancel out, and overtime is counted only **once, at the end of the period**, on the hours exceeding the period average (e.g. 1607h/yr, or 35h × #weeks). Lets a business flex week to week without paying weekly overtime, as long as the average holds at 35h. | Period cap partially (#34-style); genuine multi-week cycle averaging unconfirmed | 🟠 | L3121-41 to -47; F75 | in-scope (A) · M/L |
| 38 | **Horaires individualisés / report d'heures** (flexitime) | Employees pick their own start/finish times around core hours; the hours they run over or under in a week are carried into the next week as a running **credit/debit** balance instead of being settled at once. By default an employee may carry at most **+3h** week-to-week, and the running balance is capped at **10h**. Hours carried this way are **not** overtime — overtime exists only on hours the employer actually requested beyond the schedule. | Tolerance/bank might carry ±hours; a crédit/débit balance *excluded from OT* is unconfirmed | 🟠 | L3121-48 ff.; F74 | in-scope (A) · M |
| 39 | **Récupération des heures perdues** | When work stops for the whole team for a specific reason — bad weather / force majeure, a stock-taking inventory, or a "bridge" closure of 1–2 days between a holiday and the weekly rest — those lost hours may be **made up later at the normal rate, NOT as overtime**. Recovery is capped at **+1h/day and +8h/week** and must fall within the 12 months before or after the loss. The engine needs a "recovered hours" bucket excluded from the weekly 35h overtime trigger. | No dedicated "recovered, non-OT" hour class | 🟠 | L3121-50/51 (verified L3121-50); code.travail | in-scope (A) · M |
| 40 | **Repos compensateur de nuit** | Every recognised night worker (#27) must **by law receive compensatory REST** for working nights — separate from, and on top of, any night pay premium (#11). The Code sets the obligation (L3122-8) but not the amount: the applicable agreement fixes it (a common figure is ~20 min rest per week worked on nights, +20% for workers aged 57+). The rest is **compulsory** — the employer cannot swap it for money unless the agreement explicitly allows. | Banked-hours rest-in-lieu like #9; auto per-night-hour accrual unconfirmed | 🟠 | L3122-8; CCN | in-scope (A) · M |
| 41 | **Heures au-delà du contingent (hors contingent)** | Once a worker passes the annual overtime allowance (the 220h contingent, #5), any further overtime is still paid with its majoration **and** additionally entitles the worker to mandatory compensatory rest (COR, #8). This is a *clarifying overlap*, not a separate mechanism — it is simply #5 (cap) + #8 (COR) acting together. (The older individually-agreed "heures choisies" variant is now largely obsolete.) | Period cap (#5) + COR (#8) machinery largely covers it | 🟡 | L3121-30 (contingent); L3121-38 (COR) | in-scope (A) · S |
| 42 | **Forfait hebdomadaire / mensuel en heures** | Instead of pay being computed hour-by-hour, the contract fixes a lump **package of hours per week or per month** — e.g. 39h/week — with a matching salary that already bakes in the overtime majoration for the hours above 35h inside the package. Overtime arises only on hours worked *beyond* the forfait. Any employee can sign a weekly/monthly hours-forfait (the *annual* one, #34, is reserved for autonomous cadres); pay must never fall below hours + legal majorations. | #15/#34 note we don't count monthly/yearly OT → weekly/monthly forfait likely unsupported | 🟠 | L3121-56 | in-scope (A) · M |
| 43 | **1 May (Fête du Travail) worked → +100%** | 1 May is the only public holiday the law forces to be **both paid and not-worked** for everyone. Where the business must work it (hospitals, hotels, transport…), the employee is **paid double** — normal pay for the day PLUS an equal amount again (L3133-6). A specific statutory holiday premium that #13 records the *day* for but does not itself price. | Holiday rate rows (#13/#14) can carry a +100% band on 1 May | ✅ | L3133-4 to -6 | in-scope (A) · S |
| 44 | **Dérogation au repos quotidien (<11h)** | The 11-hour minimum rest between two working days (#12) is not absolute: a collective agreement — or a decree for certain activities (continuous operations, split-shift jobs, urgent work) — may lower it, **but never below 9 hours**. The engine needs the daily-rest minimum to be *configurable* rather than hard-wired to 11h. | crossShiftsInterval (#12) configurable to a lower minimum | 🟡 | L3131-2; D3131-1 ff. | in-scope (A) · S |
| 45 | **Congés pour événements familiaux** | The law grants set numbers of **paid days off for family events**, and these count as time worked (no dent to leave or seniority): **4 days** for the employee's own marriage/PACS, **3** for a birth/adoption, **1** for a child's marriage, **3** for the death of a close relative (spouse, parent, sibling…), and **12–14** for the death of a child. | 46 | **Jours de fractionnement** | If an employee **splits** their main annual leave so part of it falls *outside* the 1 May–31 Oct window, they earn bonus paid-leave days. With the conditions met (≥12 continuous days taken in season, and ≥3 taken out of season), they gain **1 extra day for 3–5** out-of-season days, or **2 extra for 6+**. A conditional accrual driven by *when* leave is taken, not how much. | CP accrual exists (#20); the date-conditional bonus likely needs a rule/manual step | 🟠 | L3141-19 ff. | in-scope (B) · M |
| 47 | **Compte épargne-temps (CET)** | A "time-savings account" lets an employee **park** unused paid leave, RTT days, or overtime into a personal balance and draw on it much later — to fund a long leave, an early retirement, a training period, or (sometimes) to be paid out as money. | 48 | **Pause d'allaitement** | For **one year after the birth**, a breastfeeding employee is entitled to **1 hour per working day** to nurse, normally split into two 30-min breaks (reduced to 20 min each where the employer provides a nursing room); she may nurse on the premises. **Note:** by statute this hour is *not* counted as working time and is *unpaid*, unless the applicable collective agreement provides otherwise. | Paid/unpaid short-absence or break code (like #25) | ✅ | L1225-30 ff.; R1225-5 | in-scope (B) · S |
| 49 | **Temps d'habillage/déshabillage** | When a worker is **required** to put on a uniform / protective gear **and** to do so at the workplace, the time spent dressing and undressing must be **compensated — either paid or given back as rest** — even though it isn't necessarily counted as working time. | 50 | **Heures de délégation** | Elected staff representatives (CSE) and union delegates get a **monthly credit of hours** to run their mandate, and those hours are **paid and counted as normal working time** (with a legal presumption they were used properly).
