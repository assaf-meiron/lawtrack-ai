# Japan — T&A requirements

> **What this is.** The ground-truth reference for Japan's time-&-attendance legal requirements,
> detailed enough to **build a day.io pay policy from**. It aims to be **exhaustive** and **atomic**:
> **one legal proposition per row**, each row self-contained (no "see §X" as the only content), with
> exact values, a worked example wherever a number is involved, variants, and a `Basis` that **links
> to the primary source** so any row can be checked against the law directly.
>
> **Scope: time & day-events only** — money (%, ¥, tax, gross-to-net) is downstream *context* here
> (premiums are named for context in `Values`, but the deliverable is always the typed hour/day
> event, never the money).
> **Japanese-term convention:** every non-English term is glossed in English in brackets on first
> use in each section, e.g. *zangyō* [overtime].
>
> **The one structural fact that shapes this whole document.** Japan is **statute-led and uniform
> nationwide** — the **Rōdō Kijun Hō** [Labor Standards Act, LSA] fixes working-time limits, the
> mandatory overtime-premium *rates*, rest, breaks and leave **directly, in the statute itself**.
> Unlike Germany's *Tarifvertrag* or Brazil's *CCT* layer, there is **no CBA/award layer that resets
> these numbers** — a Tokyo and an Osaka employer owe the identical figures. The one real
> configurable lever is the **Article 36 Agreement** [*Saburoku Kyōtei*, 三六協定] — a
> labour-management agreement every employer must file to permit *any* overtime or statutory-holiday
> work at all — which sets workplace-specific *ceilings* (never the rate; the rate is fixed by
> statute).
>
> **Legal sources & links.** Primary source is the **Rōdō Kijun Hō** [Labor Standards Act, LSA, Act
> No. 49 of 1947] via its official English translation on
> [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) (a
> single-page rendering of the full Act — confirmed live; article numbers are visible but not
> individually anchored, so `Basis` links point to this one page for every LSA article), plus its
> Enforcement Ordinance, the **Ikuji Kaigo Kyūgyō Hō** [Act on Childcare Leave, Caregiver Leave, and
> Other Measures for the Welfare of Workers Caring for Children or Other Family Members] at
> [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/81](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/81),
> the Industrial Safety and Health Act at
> [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3440/en](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3440/en),
> the Part-Time/Fixed-Term Worker Act at
> [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3499/en](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3499/en),
> MHLW (Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare) guidelines, and reputable multi-country/local-firm
> guides (L&E Global, Ius Laboris, JETRO, Monolith Law, TokyoDev) — full list in Sources at the foot.
> 🔎 marks a figure or deep link not independently confirmed in this pass.

## 1. Scope, classification & governing sources

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Statute sets both limits and pay, nationwide** | Unlike Germany or Brazil, the **LSA fixes the OT premium rate directly** in the statute (see §3b) — there is no CBA/award layer resetting it, and no state/prefecture variation. | A Tokyo and an Osaka employer owe the identical 25% OT premium — no regional variance. | — | [LSA Art. 37](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Article 36 Agreement (36 Kyōtei) — the gate for any overtime** | Employers **must** file a labour-management agreement (with the majority union, or a majority-elected worker representative) with the local Labour Standards Inspection Office before requiring **any** hours beyond the statutory 8h/day·40h/week, or **any** work on a statutory rest day. Without a filed 36 Agreement, ordering OT is unlawful regardless of pay. | A firm with no 36 Agreement on file cannot lawfully ask anyone to stay past 17:00 on an 8h-day schedule, even offering 125% pay. | Some very small/seasonal exemptions exist; the special-clause variant lifts the standard caps (see §3c). | [LSA Art. 36](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Statutory working-hour ceiling (default) — 8h/day·40h/week** | **8h/day · 40h/week.** | A 9-person clinic on the default regime cannot schedule past 8h/day·40h/week without OT tracking. | 44h/week special ceiling for retail/service/healthcare establishments with **<10 employees** (next row). | [LSA Art. 32](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **44h/week special ceiling — small commerce/health/hospitality** | Establishments with **<10 employees** in commerce, theatres/entertainment, health & hygiene, or hospitality may run a **44h/week** ceiling instead of 40h. | A 9-person clinic may schedule up to 44h/week without triggering OT before the 41st hour. | — | [LSA Art. 40](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); [LSA Enforcement Ordinance 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |
| **Managerial/supervisory exemption (Kanri Kantoku-sha)** | Employees who genuinely (1) hold significant duties/authority integral to management (hiring, firing, evaluating), (2) are **not** strictly time-managed, and (3) are compensated commensurate with the role are **exempt from working-hour, break and rest-day rules** — but **not** from the night-work premium. All 3 tests must hold; job title alone is insufficient. | A store "manager" with no hiring/firing authority and a schedule set like rank-and-file staff (as in the 2008 Japan McDonald's ruling) is **not** exempt, despite the title, and is owed back OT. | Widely misapplied ("manager in name only," *nabaka kanrishoku*) — a major litigation area. | [LSA Art. 41(2)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); Japan McDonald's case, Tokyo Dist. Ct. 2008 🔎 [courts.go.jp](https://www.courts.go.jp/app/hanrei_en/search) |
| **High-Level Professional System (Kōdo Professional, 高プロ)** | Fully exempts from working-hour, break, rest-day **and** OT/night-premium rules for specified high-skill roles (traders, analysts, consultants, R&D) earning **≥¥10.75M/year**, via a labour-management committee resolution filed with the Labour Standards Inspection Office. Employer must still **track** actual hours worked (health-safeguard duty) even though pay isn't hour-based. | A securities analyst earning ¥12M/year under a registered 高プロ scheme draws salary regardless of hours worked, but the employer must still log entry/exit times for health-check triggers. | Narrow population; committee resolution + individual consent required. | [LSA Art. 41-2](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) (effective April 2019) |
| **Discretionary work system (Sairyō Rōdō-sei, 裁量労働制)** | For specified professional or planning/research roles where the worker controls their own method and hours: parties **deem** a fixed number of hours worked per day (agreed in advance) regardless of actual clock time; OT premium applies only if the **deemed** hours exceed 8h/day. Requires a labour-management agreement, renewable up to a multi-year term. | A deemed-8.5h/day agreement means the worker is paid for 8.5h (with 0.5h OT premium) every working day, whatever their actual hours were. | Professional type (19 designated occupations: R&D, designers, systems analysts, journalists, etc.) vs. planning/policy type (broader eligibility, needs individual consent). | [LSA Art. 38-3, 38-4](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Variable working-hour system — 1-month unit** | Average ≤8h/day·40h/week tested over the **1-month** settlement period; no daily/weekly OT until the monthly total (against the averaged figure) is exceeded. Requires a written labour-management agreement, filed. | A worker logs 45h in week 1 and 35h in week 3 of the settlement month; only the month-end total against the period average determines OT owed. | — | [LSA Art. 32-2](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Variable working-hour system — 1-year unit** | Average ≤8h/day·40h/week tested over up to **1 year**, with sub-caps: daily **≤10h**, weekly **≤52h**, ≤52 weeks, ≤280 working days/year (for periods >3 months), ≤6 consecutive workdays (12 in a defined busy period), ≤3 consecutive weeks >48h. | A theme park on the 1-year variable system schedules 10h summer days offset by <8h winter days, keeping the annual average ≤40h/week. | See §3d and §4 for how the sub-caps feed the OT/limit tests. | [LSA Art. 32-4](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Variable working-hour system — weekly unit** | Retail/hospitality/food-service establishments with **<30 employees** may set daily hours day-by-day (up to **10h/day**) against a **≤40h/week** ceiling, via written labour-management agreement. | A small restaurant assigns 10h on a Friday and 6h on a Tuesday within the same ≤40h week. | Available only to qualifying small establishments (<30 employees, listed sectors). | [LSA Art. 32-5](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Flextime system (Furekkusu Taimu-sei)** | Employees choose daily start/end times within a **settlement period ≤3 months** (previously capped at 1 month before the 2019 reform); average must stay ≤40h/week (44h/week for special-ceiling establishments) over the period; OT is calculated **only at period-close** against the period total, not daily. | A worker logs 45h one week and 35h the next inside a 3-month flex period; no OT is owed unless the whole period's average tops 40h/week. | Core-hours band is a company design choice (not a statutory requirement). | [LSA Art. 32-3](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Part-time & fixed-term worker equal-treatment** | The Part-Time/Fixed-Term Worker Act bars **unreasonable disparity** in working conditions (incl. OT/premium treatment) between part-time/fixed-term and full-time regular workers doing comparable work. | A part-timer doing identical duties to a full-timer must receive the same OT-premium rate structure once scheduled hours exceed 8h/day·40h/week. | — | [Part-Time/Fixed-Term Worker Act Art. 8, 9](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3499/en) |
| **Multiple job-holders — combined-hours test (2020 guideline)** | Where a worker holds concurrent jobs, working hours are **aggregated across employers** (self-reported) against the 8h/day·40h/week statutory ceiling; whichever employer's hours push the *combined* total over the ceiling owes the OT premium for the hours worked at their own workplace. A simplified "management model" lets employers pre-agree side-job hour caps to avoid combined tracking. | An employee works 6h at Employer A then 3h at Employer B same day: the 9th combined hour (at Employer B) is OT, payable by Employer B. | Employer-to-employer reporting burden; "management model" is an opt-in simplification. | MHLW Guidelines for Promotion of Side Work (rev. 1 Sep 2020) 🔎 [mhlw.go.jp](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/) |
| **Minors 15–17 — hours ceiling and OT/holiday-work ban** | May work **≤8h/day · ≤40h/week** (or, by ordinance, up to 48h/week with a daily cap of 8h under limited conditions 🔎); **no overtime and no statutory-holiday work permitted** under any 36 Agreement variant. | A 17-year-old retail clerk cannot be assigned any OT hour, even with a 36 Agreement covering the rest of the workforce. | Entertainment-industry minors and agriculture/fisheries carry narrow carve-outs. | [LSA Art. 60](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Children under 15 — general prohibition** | Employment barred until **31 March following the child's 15th birthday** (end of compulsory education); exception for **light, non-hazardous work** from age 13 (age 12 in film/entertainment) with Labour Standards Inspection Office permission. | A 13-year-old may be permitted light non-industrial work (e.g. a paper round) with official approval; a factory job is barred outright. | Film/entertainment industry: age-12 exception. | [LSA Art. 56](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Foreign / Specified Skilled Worker visa holders** | T&A rules apply **identically** regardless of nationality/visa status — no separate hours regime, though visa category may gate the *job* itself (money/status, downstream). | — | — | [LSA Art. 3](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) (nationality non-discrimination) |
| **⚠ Pending — mandatory inter-shift rest system** | The inter-shift rest interval (see §4) is currently an **effort obligation** (努力義務, *doryoku gimu*) only — **not yet a hard legal requirement**. Legislative discussion targets a possible **2027** mandate. **Do not model as settled.** | A firm scheduling an 8h inter-shift gap today breaches no hard rule; the same gap could become a violation if the discussed 2027 mandate (9–11h floor) takes effect. | — | [Act on Improvement of Working Conditions (as amended) 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search); [MHLW discussion, 2026 🔎](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/) |

## 2. Working-time definition

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **What counts as working time** | Time under the employer's direction/control, including mandatory waiting, standby, and training the employer requires. Time the worker is genuinely free to use is not working time. | Waiting on the sales floor for a customer counts as working time even with no active task. | — | [LSA Art. 32](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); MHLW working-hour guidelines 🔎 |
| **Accurate, objective time capture (2019 guideline)** | Employers must record working hours **objectively** (PC login/logout, IC-card, biometric, or time clock) rather than rely solely on employee self-report; "accurate recording" reaches down to the **minute**. | A firm relying only on handwritten self-reported timesheets, with no corroborating system log, fails the guideline. | Small enterprises may combine self-report with employer confirmation where an automated system is genuinely impracticable. | MHLW "Guidelines for Employers to Appropriately Manage Working Hours" (2017/2019) 🔎 [mhlw.go.jp](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/); [LSA Art. 109](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **On-site standby/duty (Shukuchoku/Nichoku) — permitted exemption** | Intermittent night/day-duty (e.g. facility watch duty) can be exempted from ordinary hour limits **only** with prior Labour Standards Inspection Office approval; still carries the **25% late-night premium** if it falls 22:00–05:00. | A building's night watchman on an approved shukuchoku permit is not owed ordinary OT for the duty hours, but is owed the night premium for the 22:00–05:00 portion. | Approval is duty-specific and revocable if the work proves not genuinely intermittent/light. | [LSA Enforcement Ordinance Art. 23 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |
| **Combined-employer working time (multiple job holders)** | Hours are aggregated **across all employers** for the 8h/day·40h/week test (see §1); each employer's own schedule is evaluated against the running combined total. | A worker logs 5h at Employer A then 4h at Employer B: the combined 9th hour (at Employer B) is OT, owed by Employer B, even though neither employer alone scheduled 8h+. | See §1 "management model" opt-in. | MHLW Side-Work Guidelines (2020) 🔎 [mhlw.go.jp](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/) |

## 3. Overtime

*Japan is the rare jurisdiction where the statute fixes both the OT **onset** (statutory 8h/day·40h/week baseline) and the **rate** (25%/50% bands) directly — no CBA layer resets these numbers.*

### 3a. Onset / trigger

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Statutory onset — 8h/day or 40h/week** | *Zangyō* [overtime] = hours worked beyond **8h in a day** or **40h in a week**, whichever trigger is hit first, once a valid 36 Agreement is on file. | An 8.5h day triggers 0.5h of daily OT even if the week totals only 35h. | 44h/week special-ceiling establishments shift the weekly trigger to 44h. Variable/flex systems shift the test to the period average (see §1, §3d). | [LSA Art. 32, 36, 37](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Deemed-hours onset (discretionary work)** | For discretionary-work agreements, OT is measured against the **agreed deemed daily hours**, not actual clock time. | A worker deemed to work 9h/day is paid 1h of OT premium every working day, regardless of when they actually left. | — | [LSA Art. 38-3, 38-4](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **No 36 Agreement = no lawful OT, at any rate** | Absent a filed Article 36 Agreement, requiring work beyond 8h/day·40h/week (or any statutory-holiday work) is itself unlawful — a pay premium does not cure it. | A firm with no 36 Agreement offering 200% pay to stay late is still acting unlawfully — the defect is the missing filing, not the rate. | — | [LSA Art. 36](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Combined-employer onset (side jobs)** | The 8h/day·40h/week trigger is evaluated on the **combined** hours across all of a worker's employers (see §1/§2); the employer whose hours push the worker over the line owes OT for hours worked at that employer. | A worker at 6h Employer A + 3h Employer B: the 9th combined hour, worked at Employer B, is OT owed by Employer B. | "Management model" pre-agreed caps avoid the combined calculation. | MHLW Side-Work Guidelines (2020) 🔎 [mhlw.go.jp](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/) |

### 3b. Rate & bands

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard OT premium — 25%** | **≥25%** uplift on the ordinary hourly rate for all hours beyond the statutory 8h/day·40h/week trigger. | A worker earning ¥2,000/h ordinary rate who works 1h of OT is owed at least ¥2,500 for that hour (context; the T&A deliverable is the OT-hour event). | Employer may set a higher voluntary rate; cannot set lower. | [LSA Art. 37(1)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **High-volume OT premium — 50% above 60h/month** | **≥50%** uplift for OT hours **in excess of 60h in a calendar month** (holiday-work hours count toward the 60h test only for hours also exceeding statutory limits; late-night hours add on top — see §3e). Applies to **all employer sizes** since 1 April 2023 (previously large employers only). | A worker with 75h of monthly OT: the first 60h at ≥25%, the remaining 15h at ≥50%. | Employers may substitute **paid time off in lieu** for the incremental 50%-band premium if a labour-management agreement permits (see §7). | [LSA Art. 37(1) proviso](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); 2023 SME extension 🔎 |
| **Statutory-holiday work premium — 35%** | **≥35%** uplift for hours worked on a **statutory rest day** (see §6) — a separate premium type from ordinary OT, not additive to the 25%/50% OT bands (holiday-work hours are their own bucket). | A full 8h shift worked on the statutory weekly rest day is paid at ≥135% for all 8h, not 125%. | — | [LSA Art. 37(1)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Late-night premium — 25%** | **≥25%** uplift for hours worked **22:00–05:00**, stacking additively with whichever other premium(s) apply (see §3e, §5). | A night-shift OT hour (22:30, already past the 8h trigger) is paid at ≥150% (25% OT + 25% night, both on the base). | — | [LSA Art. 37(4)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **No premium during managerial/High-Level Professional exemption** | Exempt managerial staff (§1) draw **no** OT/holiday premium (night premium still applies to managers, not to 高プロ staff). | — | — | [LSA Art. 41, 41-2](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

### 3c. Caps & counters

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard monthly/annual OT ceiling — 45h/month · 360h/year** | Under an ordinary Article 36 Agreement, OT may not exceed **45h in a month** or **360h in a year** (a running cross-run counter). | A worker at 44h of OT this month is still within cap; 46h breaches it. | 1-year variable-hour-system workers: tighter ceiling, **42h/month · 320h/year**. | [LSA Art. 36(4)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Special-clause extension — up to 720h/year, 100h/single month, 80h/2–6-month average** | A **special-provision 36 Agreement** [*tokubetsu jōkō*] permits temporary excess for unforeseeable spikes: **≤720h OT/year**; any **single month** (OT + statutory-holiday work combined) **<100h**; the **2-to-6-month rolling average** (OT + holiday work) must stay **≤80h**; the 45h/month ceiling may be exceeded **no more than 6 months per year**. | A firm hits a Q4 surge: October at 90h OT is lawful only if the Jul–Dec (6-mo) rolling average of OT+holiday-work stays ≤80h/month and October is one of ≤6 months/year over 45h. | Breach of any one of these four sub-limits is a criminal offence for the employer (LSA penal provisions). | [LSA Art. 36(5)-(6)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) (2019 Work Style Reform) |
| **Mandatory medical consultation trigger — >80h/month** | Employer must offer/arrange a **physician interview** [*ishi men-setsu shidō*] once a worker's OT (calculated on request or per fatigue-accumulation rules) exceeds **80h/month** — a health-safeguard counter, not a pay rule. | A worker logging 85h OT in a month must be offered a doctor consultation regardless of whether they request it. | Karōshi [death from overwork] compensation claims commonly reference the 80h/100h thresholds as the recognised overwork benchmark. | [Industrial Safety and Health Act Art. 66-8](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3440/en) |
| **Minors — no OT, no exceptions** | Workers under 18 may **not** be scheduled any OT hours nor statutory-holiday work under any 36 Agreement variant. | A 17-year-old apprentice cannot be assigned even 1h of OT, regardless of a filed 36 Agreement covering the rest of the workforce. | Narrow exceptions for supervised on-the-job training in specific sectors 🔎. | [LSA Art. 60(1)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

### 3d. Averaging & annualisation

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Variable-hour-system averaging determines OT, not just limits** | Under the 1-month, 1-year, or weekly variable systems and flextime (see §1), OT is **determined at period close** against the period-average 8h/day·40h/week test — a day/week that exceeds 8h/40h is **not** itself OT if the pre-set schedule assigned longer hours and the period average holds. | Under a 1-year variable system, a scheduled 10h July day is **not** OT; OT is only what remains once the full annual average is tested. | Flextime settlement period ≤3 months (post-2019 reform; previously ≤1 month). | [LSA Art. 32-2, 32-3, 32-4, 32-5](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **1-year system — sub-period consecutive-week cap feeds the OT test** | No more than **3 consecutive weeks** may exceed 48h within the annual period (for periods >3 months this rises to a per-quarter cap); breach reopens the OT calculation for the excess. | A 1-year variable-hour worker logs 50h in weeks 1–4 (4 consecutive weeks >48h): the 4th week breaches the 3-consecutive-week cap, and its excess hours are recalculated as OT rather than absorbed into the annual average. | — | [LSA Enforcement Ordinance 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |

### 3e. Composition & stacking

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Premiums stack additively across independent triggers** | OT (25%/50%), statutory-holiday (35%), and late-night (25%) premiums are each **separately triggered** and **add** onto the base rate when their conditions co-occur — night work during OT stacks OT+night; night work on a statutory holiday stacks holiday+night (but holiday work itself is never additionally "OT" — see §3b). | A night-shift OT hour at 23:00 (already past 8h/day): base + 25% (OT) + 25% (night) = **≥150%**. A statutory-holiday night hour: base + 35% (holiday) + 25% (night) = **≥160%**. | The >60h/month 50% band replaces (does not add to) the standard 25% OT band for those incremental hours. | [LSA Art. 37(1), (3), (4)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Typed buckets for payroll** | Ordinary OT hours, >60h/month OT hours, statutory-holiday hours, and late-night hours are tracked as **separate typed totals**, since each carries an independently-set uplift %. | — | — | [LSA Art. 37](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

## 4. Rest, breaks & working-time limits

*Hard limits → flag a breach, don't silently cap hours.*

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Daily statutory ceiling — 8h** | **≤8h/day** default; extendable only via a valid 36 Agreement (becomes tracked OT beyond this, see §3). | A worker scheduled 8h Mon–Fri hits the ceiling exactly; a 9h Tuesday requires a filed 36 Agreement and produces 1h of tracked OT. | 44h/week special-ceiling establishments; variable-hour systems raise the daily figure to 10h under their own averaging test (§3d). | [LSA Art. 32](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Weekly statutory ceiling — 40h** | **≤40h/week** default. | A 5×8h schedule totals exactly 40h/week; a 6th 8h day (48h total) requires a filed 36 Agreement and produces 8h of tracked OT. | 44h/week for qualifying small commerce/hospitality/health establishments. | [LSA Art. 32, 40](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Breaks by hours worked** | **≥45 min** for 6–8h worked; **≥60 min** for **>8h** worked; must be given **during** the shift (not tacked on at start/end) and, in principle, **simultaneously** to all workers at an establishment (waivable by labour-management agreement). | An 8h-flat shift needs no statutory break trigger at exactly 8h, but a shift of 8h01 needs ≥60 min. | Transport, retail (customer-facing) and a few sectors carry simultaneity waivers by ordinance. | [LSA Art. 34](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Weekly rest day — 1 day/week or 4 days/4 weeks** | **≥1 rest day per week**, or (for irregular-schedule employers) **≥4 rest days per any 4-week period**. | A hospitality worker under the 4-week variant might work 10 straight days if ≥4 rest days land somewhere across the 4-week block. | The 4-week variant requires the 4-week block to be fixed in the work rules in advance. | [LSA Art. 35](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Inter-shift rest interval — not yet mandatory** | **No hard statutory minimum** rest between shifts today; the Act on Improvement of Working Conditions imposes only an **effort obligation** (努力義務) with **9h as a floor guideline** and **11h recommended** (aligning to the EU standard). A hard mandate is under discussion for **~2027 — not yet law**. | A firm scheduling an employee off at 23:00 and back at 07:00 (8h gap) breaches no current hard rule, only the soft guideline. | Certain sectors (e.g. hire-truck drivers under the "2024 problem" reforms) already carry sector-specific **mandatory** interval rules (typically 9–11h) — a narrower, sector-specific hard mandate predating the general one. | [Act on Improvement of Working Conditions (as amended) 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search); [Improvement Standards for driver-hours (transport sector) 🔎](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/) |
| **1-year variable system — extra ceilings** | Daily **≤10h**, weekly **≤52h**, ≤3 consecutive weeks >48h, ≤6 (or 12, in a defined peak) consecutive workdays, ≤280 working days/year (periods >3 months). | A theme-park worker scheduled 10h/day for 6 straight summer days is at the consecutive-workday limit; a 7th straight 10h day breaches the ≤6-day cap (absent a declared peak period allowing 12). | See §1, §3d. | [LSA Art. 32-4](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); [LSA Enforcement Ordinance 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |
| **Minors — no averaging relief** | The 8h/day·40h/week ceiling for under-18s is **firm** — none of the variable-hour/flextime averaging systems apply to reduce protection below this baseline, and no OT/holiday work is layered on. | A 17-year-old cannot be placed on the company's 1-year variable-hour schedule to work 10h summer days — the flat 8h/day cap still applies to them even though adult colleagues average it out. | — | [LSA Art. 60](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Managerial/High-Level Professional exemption from all limits above** | Genuinely qualifying *kanri kantoku-sha* and 高プロ staff sit **outside** the daily/weekly ceilings, break rule, and rest-day rule entirely (see §1). | — | — | [LSA Art. 41, 41-2](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

## 5. Night & shift work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |

| **Night window — 22:00–05:00** | Late-night hours are **22:00–05:00**, carrying the **≥25%** premium (see §3b/§3e) for **all** workers including managerial-exempt staff. | A manager working 23:00 still draws the night premium even though exempt from OT/holiday premiums. | — | [LSA Art. 37(4)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

## 6. Weekend & public-holiday work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **National holiday calendar — 16 days (17 in some years via "citizen's holiday" rule)** | 16 national holidays/year: New Year's Day (1 Jan), Coming-of-Age Day (2nd Mon Jan), National Foundation Day (11 Feb), Emperor's Birthday (23 Feb), Vernal Equinox Day (~20/21 Mar), Shōwa Day (29 Apr), Constitution Memorial Day (3 May), Greenery Day (4 May), Children's Day (5 May), Marine Day (3rd Mon Jul), Mountain Day (11 Aug), Respect-for-the-Aged Day (3rd Mon Sep), Autumnal Equinox Day (~22/23 Sep), Sports Day (2nd Mon Oct), Culture Day (3 Nov), Labor Thanksgiving Day (23 Nov). | 2026 calendar sets these 16 fixed/floating dates against the Gregorian calendar for scheduling. | A "citizen's holiday" [*kokumin no kyūjitsu*] adds a bonus day when an ordinary weekday falls between two holidays (next row). | Act on National Holidays (国民の祝日に関する法律) 🔎 [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |
| **"Citizen's holiday" bonus-day rule** | A weekday that falls **between two national holidays** with no holiday of its own becomes a bonus holiday [*kokumin no kyūjitsu*]. | 2026: a weekday between Marine Day and Mountain Day, or the Sept equinox cluster, can add a "Silver Week" bonus holiday via this rule. | *Obon* (mid-Aug) is a **customary**, not statutory, observance — many firms close voluntarily but it carries no legal holiday status. | Act on National Holidays 🔎 [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |
| **⚠ National holidays are NOT automatically paid non-working days** | Unlike many jurisdictions, the LSA imposes **no general duty** to give employees the calendar public holiday off, paid or otherwise — whether it's a working day is set entirely by the employer's work rules/contract. Only the distinct **statutory rest day** (below) is a hard legal entitlement. | A retail employer can lawfully roster staff to work Culture Day (3 Nov) as an ordinary working day if the work rules designate it as such. | Public-sector/government offices do close on national holidays by separate rule; private sector is discretionary. | (no LSA holiday-pay provision); [Act on National Holidays (calendar only) 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |
| **Substitute holiday (Furikae Kyūjitsu) — pre-arranged swap** | When a holiday/rest day is **swapped in advance** for a different day off, the original day legally **becomes an ordinary working day** — so **no** holiday-work premium is owed for it (only the swapped rest day is now the legal rest day). | An employer designates in advance that Monday's rest day moves to Wednesday; working Monday draws ordinary pay, not the 35% holiday premium. | Must be arranged **before** the work is performed to avoid the premium; must still leave the weekly-rest-day minimum intact. | [LSA Art. 35](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) interpretation; MHLW guidance 🔎 |
| **Compensatory day off (Daikyū) — after-the-fact** | A day off granted **after** working a statutory rest day does **not** cancel the **35%** holiday-work premium already earned for that day — the premium is still owed even though a later day is taken off. | An employee works Sunday (statutory rest day) and takes the following Wednesday off instead: Sunday is still paid at ≥135%; Wednesday off is unpaid time (or drawn from banked hours per policy). | — | [LSA Art. 37(1)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) (unaffected by after-the-fact scheduling) |
| **Statutory-holiday work premium — 35%** | **≥35%** uplift on hours worked on the designated statutory weekly rest day (see §4/§3b) — a distinct premium type from ordinary OT. | A worker called in for a full 8h shift on their designated statutory rest day is paid at ≥135% for all 8h, separate from any weekly OT bucket. | — | [LSA Art. 37(1)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

## 7. Time banking & time-off-in-lieu

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Variable-hour-system banking (1-month/1-year/weekly)** | Hours are effectively "banked" against a pre-set schedule over the settlement period (1 month, up to 1 year, or weekly for small qualifying establishments); OT is only what remains once the whole-period average is tested (see §3d). | A 1-month variable-hour worker logs 45h in week 1 and 35h in week 3; only the month-end total against the 1-month average (not the individual weeks) determines OT owed. | See §1, §3d for the three variants' specific caps. | [LSA Art. 32-2, 32-4, 32-5](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Flextime banking — up to 3-month settlement period** | Employees carry a running hour balance within the flex period (≤3 months); shortfall/excess nets out at period close against the 40h/week (or 44h/week) average. | A worker banks +10h in month 1, works -10h relative to average in month 2, netting to zero at the 2-month mark. | Pre-2019 reform: settlement period capped at 1 month; extended to 3 months by the Work Style Reform legislation. | [LSA Art. 32-3](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **On-site intermittent duty (Shukuchoku/Nichoku) — permit-gated exemption** | With prior Labour Standards Inspection Office approval, genuinely **intermittent, light** on-site night/day duty (e.g. facility watch) is exempted from the ordinary hour ceilings; still draws the **25%** late-night premium for the 22:00–05:00 portion. | A building superintendent's approved overnight watch duty isn't counted as full working hours toward the 8h/40h ceiling, but the night-window hours still draw the premium. | Approval can be revoked if actual duties prove not genuinely light/intermittent (i.e. real work is being extracted). | [LSA Enforcement Ordinance Art. 23 🔎](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search) |

## 10. Leave & absence

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nenkyū [statutory annual paid leave] — accrual schedule** | **10 working days** after **6 months** continuous service with **≥80%** attendance; then **+1 day at 1.5 yrs, +2 at 2.5 yrs, +2 at 3.5 yrs, +2 at 4.5 yrs, +3 at 5.5 yrs, +3 at 6.5+ yrs** — reaching a **maximum of 20 days/year** at 6.5 years' tenure. | A worker at exactly 6 months with 85% attendance is granted 10 days; at 2.5 years, 14 days total. | Proportional grant for part-timers (next row). | [LSA Art. 39(1)-(2)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Proportional leave for part-timers (Hirei Fuyo)** | Applies where weekly contracted hours are **<30h** *and* (weekly days **<5** or annual days **<217**). Days/year by weekly working days at 6.5+ years' tenure: **4 days/week → 15**; **3 days/week → 11**; **2 days/week → 7**; **1 day/week → 3**. Full schedule ramps identically to the full-time table, scaled down. | A 3-day/week part-timer at 6 months gets 5 days; at 6.5+ years, 11 days. | Workers ≥30h/week get the **full** (non-prorated) schedule regardless of days/week. | [LSA Art. 39(3)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); LSA Enforcement Ordinance Art. 24-3 🔎 |
| **Mandatory 5-day minimum utilisation (2019 reform)** | Employer must ensure each employee with **≥10 days** granted actually **takes ≥5 days/year**, via employer-designated scheduling if the worker hasn't self-scheduled enough. Non-compliance risks a fine up to **¥300,000 per employee**. | An employee who has taken only 2 self-chosen days by month 10 must be assigned 3 more employer-designated leave days to reach the 5-day floor. | — | [LSA Art. 39(7)-(8)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Planned annual-leave allocation (Keikaku Nenkyū)** | Via labour-management agreement, an employer may designate the scheduling of leave **beyond the worker's own 5 free-choice days** (i.e. the portion above the mandatory 5 the worker controls). | A CBA-like agreement schedules a company-wide 3-day shutdown drawn from banked leave, leaving each worker's own 5 free days untouched. | — | [LSA Art. 39(6)](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Carryover & expiry — 2 years** | Unused Nenkyū **expires 2 years** after the grant date (general civil-claim limitation as applied to leave); no employer duty to warn (unlike Germany). | Leave granted in year 1 not used by the end of year 2 is permanently forfeited. | — | [LSA Art. 115](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **No statutory paid sick leave** | Japan has **no** statutory paid sick-leave entitlement; illness absence is typically drawn from Nenkyū. Beyond ≥4 consecutive absent days, **Shōbyō Teate Kin** [sickness & injury allowance] — a health-insurance benefit, **not** an employer wage obligation, ~⅔ of standard pay for up to 18 months — may apply (money/insurance, downstream). | A worker sick 3 days uses 3 Nenkyū days (or unpaid, if exhausted); a worker sick 3+ weeks may draw the insurance allowance from day 4. | Many mid/large employers **voluntarily** grant 5–10 paid sick days/year by company policy (not statutory). | Health Insurance Act (Shōbyō Teate Kin) 🔎 [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/search); (no LSA sick-pay provision) |
| **Menstrual leave (Seiri Kyūka)** | On request, an employer **may not require** a woman to work during a period where menstruation makes work **especially difficult** — an unpaid (statutory default) leave of unspecified duration, granted per request. | An employee requests 1 day off for menstrual difficulty; the employer must grant it but need not pay for it absent company policy. | Rarely used in practice (<10% uptake per 2022 survey) due to workplace stigma. | [LSA Art. 68](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Maternity leave (Sango Kyūgyō) — pre/post-natal** | **≤6 weeks pre-birth** (14 weeks for multiple pregnancy) **on request**; **8 weeks post-birth mandatory** (reducible to 6 weeks post-birth only if the worker requests early return **and** a doctor certifies fitness). | A worker due 1 June may request leave from ~20 April; return before 27 July requires both her request and medical sign-off. | — | [LSA Art. 65](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **Childcare leave (Ikuji Kyūgyō)** | Up to the child's **1st birthday**; extendable to **1 year 6 months**, then **2 years**, if daycare placement is unavailable. | Parents unable to secure daycare at 12 months extend to 18 months, then to 24 months if still unplaced. | — | [Act on Childcare Leave, Caregiver Leave, etc. Art. 5](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/81) |
| **"Papa Mama Ikukyū Plus" — combined-parent extension** | If **both** parents take childcare leave, the combined household coverage window extends to the child's **1 year 2 months** (vs 1 year for a single parent). | A couple splits leave so the combined household coverage runs to 14 months instead of 12. | — | [Act on Childcare Leave, Caregiver Leave, etc. Art. 9-2](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/81) |
| **Postpartum paternity leave (Shussei-ji Ikuji Kyūgyō)** | Fathers may take up to **4 weeks total within 8 weeks of birth**, splittable into **2 separate blocks**. | A father takes 2 weeks immediately after birth and a further 2 weeks at week 6. | Distinct from, and stackable with, ordinary Ikuji Kyūgyō. | [Act on Childcare Leave, Caregiver Leave, etc. Art. 9-2](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/81) (2022 reform) |
| **Short-time work for childcare (Tanjikan Kinmu)** | Employer must offer a shortened-hours option until the child turns **3**; 2025 amendments require employers to offer **≥2** flexible-work measures (incl. shortened hours) for parents of children aged **3 through elementary-school entry** (extended in 2025 to **3rd grade**). | A parent of a 2-year-old works a reduced 6h/day schedule instead of 8h. | — | Act on Childcare Leave, Caregiver Leave, etc. (2025 amendments) 🔎 [japaneselawtranslation.go.jp](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/81) |

| **Family care leave (Kaigo Kyūgyō)** | **Up to 93 days total per family member requiring care**, splittable across **up to 3 separate periods**; requires ≥1 year's service (for fixed-term workers) 🔎. ## 11. Recordkeeping & tolerance

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Objective, minute-level time capture (2019 guideline)** | Employers must record actual working hours **objectively** — PC login/logout, IC-card swipe, biometric, or automated time clock preferred over self-report — accurate **to the minute**, for **every** employee including those nominally exempt (managerial staff, 高プロ, discretionary-work) for health-safeguard purposes. | A firm relying purely on employee self-reported paper timesheets with no corroborating system fails the guideline standard. | Small enterprises where automated capture is genuinely impracticable may combine self-report with employer verification. | MHLW "Guidelines for Employers to Appropriately Manage Working Hours" (2017, reinforced 2019) 🔎 [mhlw.go.jp](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/english/) |
| **Retention — 3 years (transitional; 5 years eventual)** | Working-hour records, wage ledgers, and the employee roster must be retained **≥3 years** currently (the LSA's amended text sets a future 5-year standard, presently suspended by transitional provision — **3 years remains the operative figure**). | A worker's time records from July 2023 must still be on file in July 2026 (3 years); the employer may lawfully purge them from August 2026. | — | [LSA Art. 109](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en); LSA supplementary provisions (transitional 3-year rule) 🔎 |
| **Age-verification document retention (minors)** | Employers of workers under 18 must keep an official age-verification document (e.g. residence certificate) on file at the workplace. | — | — | [LSA Art. 57](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
| **No statutory rounding/tolerance rule** | The LSA sets **no** rounding or grace-period allowance; combined with the minute-level accuracy guideline, **any systematic rounding that shaves recorded time** (e.g. rounding down to the nearest 15/30 min) is a compliance risk, not a safe-harbor practice. | A payroll system that rounds all clock-ins down to the nearest 30 minutes understates OT and breaches the accurate-recording guideline. | Rounding **up** (in the worker's favor) or minute-for-minute capture is the safe posture; systematic downward rounding has drawn back-pay claims in practice. | MHLW working-hour guidelines 🔎; [LSA Art. 24](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) (full-payment principle) |
| **Penalty for record-keeping breach** | Fine of **up to ¥300,000** for violations of the Art. 109 record-retention duty. | An employer found to have discarded time records after 18 months (short of the 3-year duty) faces a fine of up to ¥300,000 per violation on inspection. | — | [LSA Art. 120](https://www.japaneselawtranslation.go.jp/en/laws/view/3567/en) |
