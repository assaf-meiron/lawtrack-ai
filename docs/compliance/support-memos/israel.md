# Israel — T&A requirements

> **What this is.** The ground-truth reference for Israel's time-&-attendance legal requirements,
> detailed enough to **build a day.io pay policy from**. It aims to be **exhaustive** and **atomic**:
> **one legal proposition per row**, each row self-contained (no "see §X" as the only content), with
> exact values, a worked example wherever a number is involved, variants, and a `Basis` that **links
> to the primary source** so any row can be checked against the law directly.
>
> **Scope: time & day-events only** — money (%, NIS, tax, gross-to-net) is out of scope (premiums
> and currency figures are named for context in `Values`, but the deliverable is always the typed
> hour/day event, never the money). **Term convention:** every Hebrew term of art is glossed in
> English in brackets on first use, e.g. *Sha'ot Nosafot* [overtime hours].
>
> **The one structural fact that shapes this whole document.** Unlike Germany (statute sets limits,
> a CBA/*Tarifvertrag* sets the pay) or Australia (a thin federal floor, an Award layer that carries
> almost every number), Israel's **Hours of Work and Rest Law** [Chok Sha'ot Avoda VeMenucha] is a
> **single statute that sets both the hour limits *and* the statutory overtime premiums (125%/150%)
> directly** — no CBA is needed for the baseline; CBAs/contracts may only improve on it. Two other
> mechanisms then move the numbers *on top of* that statutory floor: **ministerial extension orders**
> [*tzavei harchava*, binding the whole economy] have shifted the *standard week itself* (43h → 42h
> in 2018 private-sector-wide; 40h in the **public sector only** from 2024), and sector/enterprise
> CBAs layer additional enhancements (extra leave days, night premiums, global-overtime
> arrangements) on top of the statutory floor. Religion is **statutorily embedded**, not merely
> accommodated — the weekly rest day and the holiday calendar are faith-linked by law (§1, §6).
>
> **Legal sources & links.** Primary law is cited to **Nevo** [nevo.co.il], Israel's standard online
> legal database, at the specific statute page where confirmed live; where a specific statute page
> could not be confirmed live in this pass, the citation links to the Nevo homepage as the register's
> stable base, flagged 🔎. Government-scheme rows cite the **National Insurance Institute** [Bituach
> Leumi] English site (btl.gov.il). No `worldwide-calculations/israel.md` seed exists yet — this file
> was built from the predecessor support-memo content plus fresh web research (2026-07-21): the
> **Hours of Work and Rest Law, 5711-1951**; **Annual Leave Law, 5711-1951**; **Sick Pay Law,
> 5736-1976**; **Sick Pay Law (Absence due to a Child's Illness), 5753-1993**; **Employment of Women
> Law, 5714-1954**; **Youth Labour Law, 5713-1953**; **Defence Service Law** (reserve-duty/*Miluim*
> protections); Ministry of Labor extension orders [*tzavei harchava*]; and reputable
> multi-country/local-firm guides (full list at the foot of the file). 🔎 marks a figure or a deep
> link to confirm in a re-verification pass.

## 2. Working-time definition

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Breaks generally not working time** | The mandatory daily break (§4) does **not** count as working time or attract pay **unless** the employee is required to remain at/near the workplace for operational reasons during it. | A retail worker who must stay at the register during her 30-minute break is paid for it; one who may leave the premises freely is not. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §20](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Night-work definition — two different statutory windows** | **Hours of Work and Rest Law**: "night work" = a shift with **≥2 hours falling within 22:00–06:00** — triggers the reduced 7-hour workday (§5). **Employment of Women Law**: "night" = an **11-hour span including 24:00–06:00** — the (wider/different) window for women's night-work consent rights. **Configure separately per purpose.** | A shift 21:00–05:00 is "night work" under the Hours law (2+ hrs in 22:00–06:00 → 7h-day rule); the same shift falls in the Women's-law night window too, but the window boundaries differ. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §2](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); [Employment of Women Law §2(b)](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/p176_001.htm) |
| **Reduced 7-hour day — three triggers** | The standard 8-hour day (6-day week) drops to **7 hours** on: (a) a night-work day; (b) the day immediately preceding the weekly rest day; (c) the day immediately preceding a holiday the employee doesn't work. | A Thursday before a Friday off (weekly rest) is capped at 7h even though it's not a night shift. | Only relevant to the 6-day-week structure; 5-day-week schedules already build the short day into the 42h split (§3a). | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §2(a)](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Overtime calculated daily/weekly, not monthly (absent agreement)** | OT is computed on a **daily** and **weekly** basis by default — **not netted over a month** unless the employer and employee agree otherwise (e.g. a "global overtime" arrangement, §1). | A worker with one long day and otherwise short days can't have the long day's OT offset against the short days at month's end absent such an agreement. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §16](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); case law 🔎 |
| **Remote/WFH work — same OT obligations** | Overtime entitlements apply to home/remote work the same as on-site work; the employer's supervision/monitoring of remote hours is separately constrained by privacy law (Amendment 13, Protection of Privacy Law, Aug 2025). | An employee logging extra hours from home while WFH accrues OT the same as if in-office. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) (general); Protection of Privacy Law Amendment 13 — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |

## 3. Overtime

*Israel's Hours of Work and Rest Law sets both the onset and the rate directly by statute — the opposite posture from Germany.*

### 3a. Onset / trigger

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Daily onset — 5-day week** | Standard day **8.6h (8h36m)** on 4 days + **7.6h (7h36m)** on the short 5th day = **42h/week**. Hours beyond the daily figure on any given day are overtime. 🔎 one source cites "9h" for the 5-day daily cap — treat 8.6h/7.6h as the better-corroborated figure (matches the 42h weekly total). | A 5-day-week worker doing 9.5h on a Tuesday (8.6h day) has **0.9h** of daily OT that day. | 6-day week: **8h/day** Mon–Thu + **7h** Friday/holiday-eve = up to 45h (pre-2018 economy-wide baseline; now less common in the private sector). | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §2](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); 2018 Extension Order 🔎 |
| **Weekly onset** | Hours beyond **42h/week** (private sector) or **40h/week** (public sector, since 2024) trigger OT **even if no single day** breached its daily threshold. | Five 8.6h days = 43h → the 43rd hour is weekly OT even though no day individually exceeded 8.6h. | Public-sector 40h baseline (§1). | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §16](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); 2024 Framework Agreement 🔎 |
| **Rest-day work is OT regardless of weekly hours already worked** | Hours worked on the weekly rest day count as overtime **unconditionally** — they are not netted against however many hours were already worked that week. | A worker who worked only 20h Sun–Thu and then works Saturday still earns the Saturday OT/rest-day premium for those hours. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §§9, 17](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **§30 trust/management exemption — no OT onset at all** | Employees within the management/personal-trust/unsupervised-hours exemption (§1) have **no** OT onset — the law doesn't apply to them. | An unsupervised department head who sets her own hours is owed no OT no matter how long she works. | Narrowly applied; substantive test, not title. | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §30](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Part-time onset = contracted hours** | For part-timers, OT is measured against their **contracted** weekly/monthly hours, prorated from the 42h (or 40h) full-time baseline. | A 21h/week (50%) contract worker doing 25h has OT from the 22nd hour. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) (general); part-time contract terms |
| **Global-overtime arrangement — displaces per-period onset only if valid** | Where the 4-condition test (§1) is met, a fixed monthly sum may substitute for hour-by-hour OT tracking; if **any** condition fails, statutory per-day/per-week onset re-applies retroactively. | An employer's flat monthly OT sum that fails the autonomy condition reverts to per-day/per-week OT, recalculated from the affected pay periods. | — | Supreme Court case law — [Nevo *Psika*](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |

### 3b. Rate & bands

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Weekday overtime — two-tier rate** | **First 2 daily OT hours: 125%** of the regular hourly wage. **3rd OT hour onward (same day): 150%.** These are statutory **minimums** — CBAs may improve, never reduce. | A worker earning NIS 60/h who works 2h OT then a 3rd: 2h × NIS 75 (125%) + 1h × NIS 90 (150%). | "Regular wage" for the base includes **all employer-paid supplements**, not base salary alone. | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §16](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Weekly-rest-day / holiday overtime — higher tiers** | Rest-day/holiday work is paid **at least 150%** as the base rest-day premium (§6); OT hours worked **on top of** the day's normal schedule on a rest day/holiday commonly run **175%** (first 2 OT hours) and **200%** (3rd+) 🔎 — some sources cite up to **250%** for holiday OT; treat 175%/200% as the better-corroborated tiered figure and confirm the ceiling with counsel. | A worker called in on Shabbat for a 10-hour shift: base 8h × 150%, then 2h × 175%. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §§16–17](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); sectoral practice |
| **No waiver of OT rights** | Employees **cannot validly waive** statutory OT entitlement or accept below-minimum compensation for it; a Supreme Court ruling confirms OT pay is an **addition to** base salary, not part of it (base salary must independently clear minimum wage). | A contract clause purporting to fold OT into a flat salary below the 4-condition global-OT test (§1) is unenforceable. | — | Supreme Court case law — [Nevo *Psika*](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
| **§30-exempt roles — no premium (no onset either)** | Trust/management-exempt employees receive no statutory OT premium (consistent with §3a — the law doesn't apply to them at all). | — | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §30](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |

### 3c. Caps & counters

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Absolute daily cap incl. OT — 12h/day** | A workday including overtime may not exceed **12 hours** total. | An 8.6h scheduled day can add at most 3.4h of OT before hitting the 12h ceiling. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §11](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Weekly overtime cap — ~15h/week under general permit** | The Ministry of Labor's **General Permit to Engage Employees in Overtime Work** [*heter klali*] caps OT at roughly **15h/week** 🔎 (some sources cite up to 16h/week — confirm the currently-in-force permit's exact figure); a **special/individual permit** [*heter meyuchad*] is required to exceed it. | A worker already at 15h of weekly OT cannot be lawfully scheduled for more without an individual permit. | Sector-specific special permits exist (security, health, essential services) 🔎. | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §§11–13](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); General Permit (Ministry of Labor, periodically renewed) — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
| **No statutory monthly/annual OT-hour ceiling** | Beyond the daily (12h) and weekly (~15h) caps, there is no separate monthly or annual OT-hours ceiling in the base law. | — | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |

### 3d. Averaging & annualisation

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No reference-period averaging determines OT** | OT is settled **daily and weekly** (§3a) — Israel has **no** 6-month/annual averaging window that nets OT the way Germany's ArbZG averaging validates a working-time limit. A month-level "global overtime" arrangement (§1) is a **contractual substitute**, not a statutory averaging mechanism. | An employer cannot point to a quiet month to offset a heavy month's OT absent a valid global-overtime agreement. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §16](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); case law 🔎 |
| **Daily-vs-weekly, higher governs (restated from §3a)** | When the two tests disagree, the employee is owed whichever yields **more** OT hours for that week. | A week with one very long day but a low weekly total is still owed OT via the daily test even though the weekly test alone would show none. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §16](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |

### 3e. Composition & stacking

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rest-day/holiday premium is independent of the weekly-hours count** | A rest-day/holiday hour carries its own premium **regardless of** how many ordinary hours were already worked that week — it is not "absorbed" into an already-satisfied 42h quota. | A worker who worked only 20h Sun–Thu still earns the full rest-day premium for Saturday hours (the same principle as §3a's rest-day-is-OT-unconditionally rule). | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §§9, 17](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **No statutory night premium — night is a *day-length* trigger, not a *pay* uplift** | Unlike Germany's Nachtzuschlag, Israeli statute attaches **no independent %** to night hours; "night work" status instead shortens *that day's* threshold to 7h (§2), pulling hours into OT **earlier**. Any night **pay** uplift is CBA/contract-based. 🔎 | A night-shift worker's 8th hour is already OT (7h day) even though no separate "night rate" applies by statute. | CBAs in shift-heavy sectors (security, healthcare, manufacturing) commonly add a contractual night differential. | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §2](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); CBA practice |

## 4. Rest, breaks & working-time limits

*Hard limits → flag a breach, don't silently cap hours.*

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Absolute daily cap incl. OT — 12h** | (Restated from §3c — a working-time limit, not just an OT-pay boundary.) No workday, OT included, may exceed **12 hours**. | A scheduler who tries to roster a 13-hour shift (even with the employee's agreement) is scheduling an unlawful shift — flag, don't silently truncate. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §11](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Weekly ceiling — 42h standard (private) / 40h (public)** | Standard week **42h** (private sector, since 2018) or **40h** (public sector, since 2024); actual weekly hours worked, OT included, are further bounded by the ~15h/week OT cap (§3c) → practical peak **≈57h/week** (42+15). | A private-sector worker's week is flagged as a limit breach once total hours (ordinary + OT) exceed 57h, even before the per-day 12h cap is separately checked. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); 2018/2024 orders 🔎 |
| **Daily rest between shifts — ≥8h** | A minimum **8 consecutive hours** of rest is required between the end of one working day and the start of the next. | A shift ending 23:00 → next shift may not start before 07:00. | 🔎 confirm whether any sectoral extension order shortens this (cf. Germany's 10h carve-out) — not identified in current research. | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) (rest-between-shifts provision) |
| **Weekly rest — ≥36h, reducible to ≥25h by regulation** | Minimum **36 consecutive hours** of weekly rest [*menucha shvu'it*]; certain regulated sectors may reduce this to **not less than 25 hours** under specific Ministry regulations. | A hospitality worker in a sector with a 25h-reduction regulation gets a shorter, but still mandated, weekly rest block. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §§7, 9](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Daily breaks by hours worked** | Workday of **≥6 hours**: minimum **45 minutes total** break, including **one continuous ≥30-minute segment**. | An 8h shift needs at least 45 total break minutes, ≥30 of them unbroken. | Break is unpaid/excluded from working time unless the employee must stay on-site (§2). | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §20](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Minors — tighter daily/weekly caps, no night work** | Youth Labour Law caps minors' hours below adult levels and bans night work entirely (exact hour figures 🔎 — the law is confirmed to impose "lower daily and weekly caps" and "fixed rest breaks," but the specific numeric caps were not confirmed in current research; do not assume adult figures apply). | An employer scheduling a 16-year-old on an adult-length shift or a night shift is out of compliance regardless of the exact numeric cap. | Age-banded (14 holiday-only light work; 15+ general); medical fitness certificate required under 18. | Youth Labour Law 5713-1953 — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
| **Pregnancy/postpartum — OT, night, weekend restrictions** | From the **5th month of pregnancy**, no OT, weekend, or night-shift assignment without **written consent + medical certificate**. For **4 months after** returning from maternity leave, no night/weekend work without written consent. | A worker in month 6 of pregnancy declines a rostered night shift — lawful, no penalty. | — | [Employment of Women Law §10](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/p176_001.htm) |

## 5. Night & shift work

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Night-work trigger (Hours of Work and Rest Law)** | A shift with **≥2 hours** falling within **22:00–06:00** is "night work" — triggers the reduced **7-hour** day (§2/§4), pulling OT onset earlier on that day. | A 21:30–05:30 shift qualifies (well over 2h inside 22:00–06:00) → treated as a 7h-cap day. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §2](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) |
| **Night definition (Employment of Women Law) — wider window** | A separate, **11-hour** night span **including 24:00–06:00**, used specifically for women's night-work consent/refusal rights (not the OT-onset trigger above). | A shift 20:00–07:00 (11h span) falls entirely within this wider window even though only part of it sits inside the narrower 22:00–06:00 Hours-law trigger. | Configure as a distinct, purpose-specific window from the Hours-law trigger. | [Employment of Women Law §2(b)](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/p176_001.htm) |
| **Women — right to decline night work** | A woman may **not be refused employment** solely for declaring, at hiring, that she cannot work nights for family reasons; conversely she cannot generally be **compelled** to work nights, subject to sector-specific exceptions. | An applicant who states at interview she cannot work nights for family reasons cannot lawfully be rejected on that basis alone. | Certain essential services/sectors carry statutory exceptions to the refusal right 🔎. | [Employment of Women Law §2(c)](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/p176_001.htm) |
| **No statutory night-pay premium** | (Restated from §3e.) Night hours shorten the day's OT-onset threshold but carry **no independent statutory %** — any night differential is CBA/contract-based. | — | Shift-heavy sector CBAs (security, healthcare, manufacturing) commonly add one. | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); CBA practice |
| **Sectoral extended-shift regimes exist outside the general law** | Some sectors run codified long-shift/on-call regimes distinct from the general statute (e.g. medical resident/intern shift-length reforms, up to ~18h shifts under 2023-era extension-order changes) 🔎 — sector-specific, not general economy practice. | A hospital resident's shift may lawfully run to ~18h under the sector's own regime, well past the general 12h cap (§4). | Confirm current in-force shift-length ceiling for the specific sector before modelling. | Sectoral extension orders / collective agreements — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |

## 6. Weekend & public-holiday work

|---|---|---|---|---|

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No general statutory hours-bank/working-time-account regime** | Unlike Germany's *Arbeitszeitkonto*, Israel has **no codified banking-of-hours system** in the general law — OT is settled daily/weekly by default (§3d); any flexible-hours or averaging arrangement is a **contractual construct**, valid only if it doesn't waive statutory minimums. | An employer cannot informally "bank" a long-day's surplus hours against a future short day without a specific, statute-compliant agreement. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm) (absence of a banking provision); case law 🔎 |

| **Flexible-hours arrangements — permitted if minimums held** | Employers may offer flexible schedules (e.g. flexitime-style arrangements) provided they **"meet legal requirements and do not waive any mandatory rights."** | A flexitime pilot allowing a 2-hour daily start window is lawful provided the 42h weekly ceiling and OT rules still apply underneath it. | — | General labour-law principle — Ministry guidance — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |

## 10. Leave & absence

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Annual leave — seniority-tiered accrual (5-day week)** | *Chofesha Shnatit* [annual leave], gross days incl. rest days: **years 1–5: 16 gross / 12 net working days**; **year 6: 18/14**; **year 7: 21/15**; **year 8: 22/16**; **year 9: 23/17**; **year 10: 24/18**; **year 11: 25/19**; **years 12–13: 26–27/20**; **year 14+: 28/20**. | An employee in year 3 accrues 12 net working days; by year 10, 18 net days. | **6-day week**: starts at **14 net working days** in years 1–5 (vs 12 for 5-day week). | [Annual Leave Law 5711-1951 §3](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/71906.htm) |
| **Accrual method & carryover** | Leave accrues **monthly through the year**. Default: use leave **within the year it accrues**; with employer consent, an employee may take as few as **7 days/year** and carry the balance into the **next 2 years**. Case law penalises employers who let balances grow indefinitely without prompting use. | An employee who takes only the mandatory 7 days each year can (with consent) carry a growing balance for up to 2 years before it must be used. | — | [Annual Leave Law §§3, 8](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/71906.htm) |
| **Termination payout** | Unused annual leave **must be redeemed** at termination (resignation or dismissal), calculated at the **final daily wage** × unused days. Employers may offset leave against a notice period only with clear advance written notice. | An employee terminated with 9 unused leave days and a final daily wage of NIS 500 is owed NIS 4,500 in leave payout on top of final wages. | — | [Annual Leave Law §§8, 13](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/71906.htm) |
| **Bereavement leave — 7 days paid** | *Avelut* [mourning]/*Shiva* [seven, the mourning period]: **7 paid days** for the death of a first-degree relative (parent, child, spouse, sibling, parent-in-law per some agreements). Not deducted from annual/sick leave balances; applies even during probation; covers deaths of relatives abroad. | An employee's parent passes away → 7 fully-paid days off, separate from annual/sick leave. | Religious minorities (Muslim, Christian, Druze) may take additional days per their own mourning customs; some CBAs expand the qualifying-relative list. | [Annual Leave Law §7](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/71906.htm) + extension order 🔎 |
| **Sick leave — accrual, tiered pay, cap** | *Dmei Machala* [sick pay]: accrues **1.5 days/month**, **18 days/year**, cumulative cap **90 days**. Pay: **Day 1 unpaid**; **Days 2–3: 50%** of daily wage; **Day 4+: 100%**. Daily wage = monthly salary ÷ 25. Requires a physician's certificate (needn't state the diagnosis). Applies to all employees incl. foreign nationals and (prorated) part-timers. | A 5-day illness: day 1 unpaid, days 2–3 at 50%, days 4–5 at 100%. | Accumulated days **do not pay out** at termination (unlike annual leave). | Sick Pay Law 5736-1976 — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
| **Child-sickness leave — 8/16/18/90 days by family status** | Parent of a child **under 16**: **8 days/year** (dual-working-parent household, non-participating spouse variant); **single parent: 16 days/year**; **parent of a child with a disability: 18 days/year**; **parent of a child undergoing oncology treatment: 90 days/year**. Pay tiers mirror ordinary sick leave (day 1 unpaid, days 2–3 at 50%, day 4+ at 100%). Two working parents may divide the days between them. | A single mother uses 5 of her 16 child-sickness days for a child's flu. | Requires medical documentation of the child's condition and care need. | Sick Pay Law (Absence due to a Child's Illness) 5753-1993 — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
| **Sick leave for parent care** | An employee may draw on their **own accrued sick-leave balance** to care for a parent with a serious medical condition, capped at roughly **6 days/year** 🔎 for this specific use (exact statutory cap not fully confirmed in current research — treat as indicative). | An employee with a 40-day accrued sick-leave balance uses up to 6 of those days in the year to care for a hospitalised parent, leaving the remainder for their own illness. | Public-sector terms may be more generous. | Sick Pay Law 5736-1976 (parent-care provision) — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
| **Maternity leave — 15 weeks paid, up to 26 total** | **15 weeks** paid leave (single birth; **26 weeks** for multiple births), funded via Bituach Leumi [National Insurance], **not employer-funded**. **Minimum 7 weeks must be taken after birth.** An **additional unpaid extension** to 26 weeks total is available for employees with **≥12 months' tenure**. Eligibility: insured **≥10 of the 14 months** preceding birth. | An employee due 1 June takes 15 paid weeks (~7 before + 8 after, or all 15 concentrated post-birth minus the mandatory pre-birth minimum where elected); with 12+ months' tenure she may extend unpaid to 26 weeks total. | — | National Insurance Law (Consolidated Version) — Maternity Insurance, [National Insurance Institute (Bituach Leumi)](https://www.btl.gov.il/English%20Homepage/Pages/default.aspx) 🔎; [Employment of Women Law](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/p176_001.htm) |
| **Paternity leave** | **1 week paid** from the **8th day** after birth, available **without** any transfer from the mother; **up to 7 weeks additional paid leave** if the mother **transfers** a corresponding portion of her own maternity entitlement. | **Nursing breaks — 1 hour/day, paid** | A nursing mother working **full-time** is entitled to **1 hour/day** of paid nursing time for **4 months** after maternity leave ends; no wage deduction, doesn't reduce seniority. | A full-time employee returning from maternity leave leaves 1 hour early (or arrives 1 hour late) daily, paid in full, for 4 months. | Where a role prohibits work while nursing, the employee may be absent without loss of seniority. | [Employment of Women Law §7(c)](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/p176_001.htm) |

## 11. Recordkeeping & tolerance

| Rule | Values | Worked example | Variants | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mandatory hours ledger** | Employers must maintain a **ledger** [*pinkas*] recording actual hours worked, rest periods, overtime, and the corresponding pay for every employee. | An employer with no hours ledger for a given employee is in breach of the recordkeeping duty regardless of whether pay was otherwise correct. | — | [Hours of Work and Rest Law §25](https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law01/P225_001.htm); Wage Protection Law 🔎 |

| **Biometric/monitoring data — privacy constraints** | Collection of biometric time-clock data and geolocation-based monitoring is constrained by the Protection of Privacy Law (Amendment 13, Aug 2025 strengthened obligations, incl. around AI-assisted HR monitoring). | An employer rolling out biometric time-clock terminals must satisfy Amendment 13's strengthened consent/security obligations before deployment. | — | Protection of Privacy Law, Amendment 13 (2025) — [Nevo](https://www.nevo.co.il) 🔎 |
